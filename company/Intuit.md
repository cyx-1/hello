# Intuit Inc. (NASDAQ: INTU)

## Company Overview

Intuit Inc. is an American multinational business software company headquartered in Mountain View, California. The company specializes in financial software, serving approximately 100 million customers worldwide. Led by CEO Sasan Goodarzi (since January 2019), Intuit's products include TurboTax, QuickBooks, Credit Karma, and Mailchimp.

**Founded:** 1983 by Scott Cook and Tom Proulx
**IPO:** March 12, 1993 (NASDAQ)
**Fiscal Year End:** July 31

## Main Products and Key Milestones

### TurboTax (Consumer Segment)

**Overview:** #1 selling tax preparation software in the United States

**Key Milestones:**
- **1993:** Intuit acquired Chipsoft (maker of TurboTax) for $225 million
- **2007:** Launched TurboTax Online
- **2019:** Introduced TurboTax Live with on-demand access to CPAs and EAs
- **2023 (Nov):** Launched GenAI-powered Intuit Assist for tax preparation
- **2024:** TurboTax Live revenue grew 17% for the year, representing ~30% of Consumer Group revenue
- **2024 (Sep):** Unveiled AI innovations allowing experts to complete taxes virtually in as little as 30 minutes

**Revenue:** $4.4B in FY2024 (7% YoY growth)

---

### QuickBooks (Small Business & Self-Employed Segment)

**Overview:** Market-leading small business accounting software with 85%+ US market share

**Key Milestones:**
- **1992:** First introduced QuickBooks DOS version
- **2001:** Launched QuickBooks Online (cloud accounting)
- **2002:** Launched QuickBooks Enterprise Solutions for medium-sized businesses
- **2013:** Rebuilt QuickBooks Online from the ground up with third-party platform capabilities
- **2014:** 624,000 QuickBooks Online subscribers
- **2015:** Surpassed 1 million small business customers
- **2017:** Exceeded 2 million QuickBooks Online customers globally
- **2022:** Launched QuickBooks Online Advanced
- **2023:** Reached 6.5 million QuickBooks Online subscribers
- **2024:** Launched Intuit Enterprise Suite for mid-market businesses

**Market Position:** QuickBooks Online revenue reached $8B in 2023, double Xero's $4.1B

---

### Mailchimp (Acquired November 2021)

**Overview:** Global leader in email marketing and automation with 13 million users

**Key Milestones:**
- **2001:** Founded in Atlanta by Ben Chestnut and Dan Kurzius
- **2007:** Founders pivoted to focus solely on Mailchimp
- **2009:** Introduced freemium model; user base grew from 85,000 to 450,000 within one year
- **2014:** Sending over 10 billion emails per month
- **2020:** Revenue reached $800 million
- **2021 (Nov 1):** Acquired by Intuit for $12 billion ($5.7B cash, $6.3B stock)
- **2022:** Launched 150+ new and updated features including SMS marketing, advanced segmentation, custom reporting
- **2024:** Previewed AI-powered revenue intelligence system

**Customer Base:** 13 million users globally, 2.4 million monthly active users, 800,000 paid customers (50% outside US)

---

### Credit Karma (Acquired December 2020)

**Overview:** Largest consumer digital finance platform with free credit scores and monitoring

**Key Milestones:**
- **2007 (Mar 8):** Founded by Kenneth Lin, Ryan Graciano, and Nichole Mustard
- **2009:** Gave away 1 millionth free credit score; secured first VC funding
- **2019:** Revenue reached nearly $1 billion (20% YoY growth)
- **2020 (Feb):** Intuit announced acquisition for $7.1 billion
- **2020 (Dec):** Acquisition completed for $8.1 billion ($3.4B cash, $4.7B stock/equity)
- **2024 (Jan):** Mint integrated into Credit Karma's net worth product line
- **2024:** Revenue grew 5% to $1.7 billion

**Member Base:**
- 130 million members in US, UK, and Canada
- 37 million monthly active users
- 88% engage on mobile devices
- Includes nearly half of all US millennials

---

### ProTax Segment

**Overview:** Professional tax preparation software for accountants

**Products:**
- **Lacerte:** Desktop tax preparation software
- **ProSeries:** Desktop tax preparation software
- **ProFile:** Canadian tax software
- **ProConnect Tax Online:** Cloud-based tax solution

**Revenue:** ~$600M in FY2024 (7% YoY growth)

**Note:** Effective August 1, 2025, Intuit combined Consumer, Credit Karma, and ProTax into a single Consumer business.

## Main Competitors and Differentiation

### H&R Block
**Market:** Tax preparation (Consumer segment competitor)
**Revenue:** ~$1.2B (vs. Intuit's $15.1B more)
**Differentiation:**
- Extensive network of physical retail locations
- Competitive pricing with free plan options
- Established brand with personal service focus
- Appeals to customers who prefer in-person assistance

### Xero
**Market:** Small business accounting (QuickBooks competitor)
**Headquarters:** New Zealand
**Differentiation:**
- Superior multi-currency support for international businesses
- Flat pricing with unlimited users included
- Strong integrations ecosystem
- User-friendly interface optimized for non-accountants
- Popular in UK, Australia, New Zealand markets

### FreshBooks
**Market:** Freelancers and small service businesses
**Differentiation:**
- Lower price point than QuickBooks
- Simple invoicing and expense tracking focus
- Ideal for Internet-based service companies
- Limited accounting features (no balance sheets)
- Best for single-person operations

### Sage
**Market:** Mid-sized to enterprise businesses
**Headquarters:** Newcastle-Upon-Tyne, England
**Revenue:** ~$16.4B less than Intuit
**Differentiation:**
- Sage Intacct for larger enterprises
- Industry-specific solutions
- Desktop-first with cloud connectivity
- Sage Security Shield fraud protection
- Comprehensive features for complex businesses

### Competitive Positioning Summary

| Competitor | Target Market | Key Differentiator | Intuit Advantage |
|------------|---------------|-------------------|------------------|
| H&R Block | Tax consumers | Physical locations, pricing | AI-powered guidance, ecosystem integration |
| Xero | International SMBs | Multi-currency, flat pricing | Market share, US dominance, integrations |
| FreshBooks | Freelancers | Simplicity, low cost | Scalability, comprehensive features |
| Sage | Enterprises | Industry-specific | Ecosystem approach, SMB focus |

### Intuit's Competitive Advantages

1. **Ecosystem Approach:** Seamless integration across payments, accounting, tax, and marketing
2. **AI Innovation:** GenAI-powered Intuit Assist across all products
3. **Customer Retention:** 95% of users engage with multiple Intuit products; multi-product users have 50% lower churn
4. **Market Dominance:** QuickBooks holds 85%+ US market share; TurboTax is #1 tax software
5. **Strategic Acquisitions:** $20B+ in acquisitions (Credit Karma $8.1B, Mailchimp $12B) expanded platform reach
