# Howmet Aerospace Inc.

**Stock Symbol:** NYSE: HWM
**Founded:** 2020 (spun off from Arconic Inc.); heritage dates to 1888 (Pittsburgh Reduction Company, later Alcoa)
**Headquarters:** Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, USA

## Overview

Howmet Aerospace Inc. is a leading global provider of advanced engineered solutions for the aerospace and transportation industries. The company manufactures highly engineered components for jet engines, fastening systems, titanium structures, and forged aluminum wheels. Howmet operates through four business segments and is the world's #1 supplier in aerospace fastening systems and forged aluminum truck wheels.

## Main Products and Business Segments

### Engine Products (50% of 2024 revenue, $3.7B)

**Investment Castings:**
- **Turbine airfoils:** Single-crystal and directionally solidified blades/vanes for hot sections
  - Materials: Nickel-based superalloys (IN718, René, CMSX-4)
  - Customers: GE Aerospace, Pratt & Whitney, Rolls-Royce, Safran
- **Structural castings:** Compressor cases, turbine frames, exhaust components
- **Rotating components:** Disks, hubs for compressors and turbines

**Forgings:**
- **Rings:** Seamless forged rings for engine casings
- **Shafts and disks:** High-strength components for rotating assemblies
- **Near-net-shape forgings:** Reducing machining costs

**Advanced Coatings:**
- **Thermal barrier coatings (TBC):** Ceramic layers protecting turbine blades
- **Environmental coatings:** Corrosion/oxidation protection
- **Wear-resistant coatings**

**Powder Metallurgy:**
- **Nickel and titanium powder production**
- **Hot isostatic pressing (HIP):** Near-net-shape parts from powder

**Key Programs:**
- GE Aerospace GE9X (Boeing 777X), LEAP (737 MAX, A320neo)
- Pratt & Whitney GTF (A320neo, A220)
- Rolls-Royce Trent XWB (A350), Pearl (G700/G800)
- Safran LEAP (co-production with GE)
- F-35 Pratt & Whitney F135 engine components
- More than 90% of all structural and rotating aero engine components can be produced by Howmet

### Fastening Systems (22% of 2024 revenue, $1.6B)

**Aerospace Fasteners:**
- **Huck brand:** High-strength structural bolts for airframes
  - Lockbolts, blind fasteners, rivets
- **Cherry Aerospace:** Blind rivets and fastening systems
- **Avdel:** Installation tooling and fastener systems
- **Alcoa Fastening Systems (AFS):** Titanium and specialty alloys

**Applications:**
- Boeing 737 MAX, 777X, 787: Structural joints, skin attachment
- Airbus A320neo family, A350: Wing-to-fuselage, control surfaces
- Gulfstream, Bombardier, Dassault business jets
- Military: F-35, F/A-18, CH-47, Black Hawk

**Commercial Transportation Fasteners:**
- **Huck commercial:** Heavy-duty truck and trailer fasteners
- **North America #1** in commercial transportation fasteners

**Key Differentiators:**
- Proprietary installation tools (reduces labor, improves reliability)
- Vibration-resistant locking mechanisms
- Weight-optimized titanium fasteners (10-50% weight savings vs. steel)

### Engineered Structures (15% of 2024 revenue, $1.1B)

**Titanium Structures:**
- **Airframe components:** Wing structures, fuselage frames, landing gear components
  - Boeing 787: Titanium wing-to-body fairings
  - Airbus A350: Titanium pylon structures
- **Engine components:** Fan blades, compressor blades/vanes
  - Pratt & Whitney GTF: Titanium fan blades
- **Nacelle structures:** Thrust reversers, fan cowls

**Manufacturing Capabilities:**
- **Titanium mill products:** Bar, billet, plate, sheet
- **Extrusions:** Complex cross-sections for airframe structures
- **Hot forming/superplastic forming:** Near-net-shape complex shapes
- **Machining:** 5-axis CNC for finished components

**Vertical Integration:**
- Titanium ingot production (TITAL acquisition, 2015)
- Melting, rolling, extrusion, forming, machining all in-house
- Supply chain control reduces lead times

**Defense Programs:**
- F-35: Bulkheads, wing structures
- F/A-18: Titanium forgings
- CH-47 Chinook: Rotor hub components

### Forged Wheels (13% of 2024 revenue, $1.1B)

**Heavy-Duty Truck Wheels:**
- **Alcoa Wheels:** Forged aluminum wheels for Class 8 trucks
  - Invented first forged aluminum truck wheel (1948)
  - 40-50% weight savings vs. steel wheels
  - Improved fuel economy (~3-5% from reduced unsprung weight)
- **Market position:** #1 globally in forged aluminum truck wheels

**Product Range:**
- **Dura-Bright® wheels:** No-polish finish (corrosion-resistant clear coat)
- **Dura-Flange® wheels:** Reinforced mounting flange for extreme-duty applications
- **LvL ONE® wheels:** Ultra-low weight for maximum payload

**Customers:**
- OEMs: Freightliner (Daimler), Peterbilt/Kenworth (PACCAR), Volvo Trucks, Mack Trucks
- Aftermarket: Fleet owners, owner-operators

**Revenue Decline (2024):**
- Forged Wheels revenue down 8% YoY due to heavy-duty truck market cyclicality
- North American Class 8 production fell ~15% (2024 vs. 2023)

## Key Milestones

- **1888:** Pittsburgh Reduction Company founded (became Alcoa in 1907)
- **1948:** Invented first forged aluminum truck wheel
- **2016:** Alcoa Inc. splits into Alcoa Corporation (upstream aluminum) and Arconic Inc. (engineered products)
- **2019:** Arconic Inc. announces plan to separate into two companies
- **2020:** Howmet Aerospace Inc. spun off from Arconic (effective April 1, 2020)
  - Arconic retained rolled aluminum products business
  - Howmet retained engine products, fasteners, structures, wheels
- **2021:** Completed acquisition of Arconic's remaining stake; fully independent
- **2022:** Net income doubles to $467M as commercial aerospace recovery accelerates
- **2023:** Revenue surpasses $6.6B; net income +63% to $763M
- **2024:** Revenue reaches $7.4B; net income surges 57% to $1.2B; commercial aerospace +20%

## Main Competitors and Differentiation

### Primary Competitors

#### 1. **Precision Castparts Corp. (PCC)** (Berkshire Hathaway subsidiary, ~$10.5B revenue)
- **Overlap:** Investment castings, forgings, fasteners, titanium structures
- **PCC's Strengths:**
  - **Largest independent aerospace foundry** (pre-Berkshire acquisition 2016, $37.2B)
  - **Vertical integration:** Titanium mill products (TIMET), superalloy ingots
  - **Sole-source positions:** Many GE, P&W engine programs
- **Howmet's Counterpunch:**
  - **Public company transparency** (PCC private under Berkshire, limited disclosure)
  - **Better margins:** Howmet 22.3% operating margin (2024) vs. PCC ~18-20% (estimated)
  - **Faster growth:** Howmet +12% revenue (2024) vs. PCC mid-single-digit growth
  - **Fastener market #1:** Howmet leads globally, PCC #2

#### 2. **MTU Aero Engines** (ETR: MTX, €7.3B revenue, ~$8.0B)
- **Overlap:** Engine components (primarily for commercial programs)
- **MTU's Strengths:**
  - **GE partnership:** Joint venture on GE9X, LEAP, GTF (MTX has workshare agreements)
  - **MRO dominance:** Largest independent engine MRO provider
  - **German engineering:** High-precision manufacturing
- **Howmet's Counterpunch:**
  - **Broader product range:** MTU focused on engine components; Howmet also fasteners, structures, wheels
  - **Titanium integration:** Howmet owns titanium supply chain; MTU buys titanium from suppliers
  - **U.S. defense access:** Howmet supplies F-35, F/A-18; MTU limited by German export controls

#### 3. **Safran** (EPA: SAF, €27.0B revenue, ~$29.5B)
- **Overlap:** Engine components (Safran is both OEM and supplier)
- **Safran's Strengths:**
  - **LEAP engine:** 50/50 JV with GE (CFM International) - Safran makes its own castings/forgings
  - **Landing gear:** Safran Landing Systems (largest globally)
  - **Nacelles/thrust reversers:** Safran Nacelles
- **Howmet's Counterpunch:**
  - **Independent supplier model:** Howmet sells to Safran, GE, P&W, RR (no OEM conflicts)
  - **Fastening systems:** Safran buys Howmet fasteners (Safran doesn't make fasteners)
  - **Cost competitiveness:** Howmet's U.S. manufacturing lower cost than Safran's French operations for some components

#### 4. **Arconic Corporation** (NYSE: ARNC, $7.1B revenue)
- **Overlap:** Aluminum mill products (but different end markets)
- **Arconic's Strengths:**
  - **Rolled products:** Aluminum sheet/plate for airframes (Boeing, Airbus)
  - **Building & construction:** Architectural products
- **Howmet's Counterpunch:**
  - **No direct competition:** Howmet focused on castings/forgings/fasteners; Arconic on rolled products
  - **Historical relationship:** Arconic spun out from same parent (2020), but different businesses
  - **Higher margins:** Howmet 22.3% operating margin vs. Arconic ~10-12%

#### 5. **Alcoa Corporation** (NYSE: AA, $10.6B revenue)
- **Overlap:** Minimal (Alcoa upstream, Howmet downstream)
- **Alcoa's Strengths:**
  - **Bauxite/alumina/aluminum production:** Primary aluminum smelting
  - **Commodity business:** Low-margin, high-volume
- **Howmet's Counterpunch:**
  - **Engineered products:** Howmet makes finished components (50%+ margins on engine parts); Alcoa sells raw aluminum (~5-10% margins)
  - **Customer proximity:** Howmet works directly with Boeing/Airbus; Alcoa sells to intermediaries

#### 6. **Consolidated Precision Products (CPP)** (Private equity-backed)
- **Overlap:** Investment castings, forgings
- **CPP's Strengths:**
  - **Emerging competitor:** Formed from roll-up of 30+ foundries (2012-2020)
  - **Private equity funding:** Warburg Pincus, Berkshire Partners
- **Howmet's Counterpunch:**
  - **Larger scale:** Howmet $3.7B engine products vs. CPP ~$2.5B (estimated)
  - **Established relationships:** Howmet 100+ year history with OEMs
  - **Public markets access:** Howmet can raise capital more easily

### Howmet Aerospace's Competitive Advantages

1. **Vertical Integration in Titanium:**
   - **TITAL acquisition (2015):** In-house titanium ingot production
   - **End-to-end control:** Melt → extrude → forge → machine all within Howmet
   - **Cost advantage:** 10-15% lower cost vs. competitors buying titanium from external suppliers
   - **Supply chain resilience:** No dependency on VSMPO-AVISMA (Russia, sanctioned post-2022)

2. **Proprietary Casting Technologies:**
   - **Single-crystal turbine blades:** Unidirectional grain structure (higher temperature capability)
   - **DirectionalSolidification:** Controlled grain orientation
   - **Advanced coatings:** Thermal barrier coatings extend blade life 2-3x
   - **R&D partnerships:** Joint programs with GE, P&W on next-gen materials

3. **Fastening Systems Market Leadership:**
   - **#1 globally** in aerospace fasteners (Huck, Cherry, Avdel brands)
   - **Installed base advantage:** 70% of Boeing/Airbus fleet uses Howmet fasteners → aftermarket/retrofit revenue
   - **Installation tool lock-in:** Proprietary Huck tooling creates switching costs

4. **Commercial Aerospace Leverage:**
   - **70% of revenue** from commercial aerospace (vs. 40-50% for PCC, MTU)
   - **Narrowbody boom:** LEAP engine (A320neo, 737 MAX) is largest engine program → Howmet supplies ~$2M of castings per aircraft
   - **Widebody recovery:** 787, A350 production ramping (Howmet content ~$3-5M per aircraft)

5. **Forged Wheels Differentiation:**
   - **Invented category (1948):** 75+ years of metallurgical know-how
   - **Brand recognition:** Dura-Bright® wheels specified by fleet customers
   - **Fuel economy ROI:** 3-5% fuel savings pays back wheel premium in <2 years

6. **Operational Excellence:**
   - **Operating margin expansion:** 17.0% (2022) → 22.3% (2024)
   - **Lean manufacturing:** 80% of facilities ISO 9001/AS9100 certified
   - **Automation:** Robotic casting cleaning, automated machining cells
