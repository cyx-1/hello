## Company History

Cognizant was founded in 1994 in Chennai, India, as Dun & Bradstreet Satyam Software (DBSS), a 76:24 joint venture between Dun & Bradstreet and Satyam Computers. The company started with 50 employees focused on implementing large-scale IT projects.

**Key Milestones:**
- **1994**: Founded as DBSS in Chennai, India
- **1996**: Became part of Cognizant Corporation after Dun & Bradstreet spin-off
- **1997**: Renamed to Cognizant Technology Solutions; headquarters moved to USA
- **1998**: IPO on NASDAQ at $10.00 per share, raising $34 million
- **2003**: Gained complete independence from IMS Health
- **2004**: Added to NASDAQ-100 Index
- **2005**: Reached $1 billion in annual revenue
- **2011**: Became a Fortune 500 company
- **2023**: Acquired Thirdera (ServiceNow partner) for $430 million
- **2024**: Acquired Belcan (engineering services) for $1.3 billion

### Cognizant's Competitive Differentiation

**1. Industry Vertical Expertise**
- Deep domain knowledge in healthcare, financial services, and manufacturing
- Recognized for complex digital consulting projects
- Strong project delivery and execution capabilities

**2. Strategic Acquisitions**
- Thirdera acquisition: Became one of ServiceNow's largest partners
- Belcan acquisition: Established leadership in aerospace & defense engineering
- Active M&A strategy with average of 4 acquisitions annually (2019-2024)

**3. AI and Automation Platform**
- Cognizant Neuro suite supports automation and AI adoption
- Built on NVIDIA AI for enterprise-grade solutions
- Proprietary solutions differentiating from peers

**4. Pricing Strategy**
- Leverages legacy as cost-effective IT services provider
- Increasingly engages on fixed-price basis
- Uses automation to maintain margins on new accounts

**5. Balanced Go-to-Market**
- Mix of partner-enabled and proprietary solutions
- Key partnerships with NVIDIA, ServiceNow, and major cloud providers
- Strong new client acquisition and large deal momentum

### Competitive Challenges

- India-centric peers (TCS, Infosys) compete aggressively on price
- Need to continuously invest in proprietary solutions to differentiate
- Restructuring programs ongoing to improve efficiency and margins
