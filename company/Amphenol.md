## Main Products and Key Milestones

### Product Portfolio
- Electronic and fiber optic connectors
- Cable and interconnect systems
- Antennas and antenna systems
- Sensors and sensor-based products
- Coaxial and high-speed specialty cable
- Military-specification circular environmental connectors

### Historical Milestones
- **1932:** Founded as American Phenolic Corp. in Chicago
- **World War II:** Primary manufacturer of military connectors; supplied 60%+ of aircraft industry connectors
- **1950s:** Invented the widely-used BNC connector
- **1957:** Listed on NYSE as Amphenol Electronics Corporation
- **1986:** Acquired Socapex (French connector manufacturer)
- **1991:** Class A Common Stock began trading on NYSE
- **2005:** Acquired Teradyne Connection Systems
- **2013:** Acquired GE's Advanced Sensors Group
- **2016:** Acquired FCI (major connector acquisition)
- **2021:** Acquired MTS Systems Corporation sensor business
- **2021:** Acquired Halo Technology Limited ($715M)
- **2024:** Acquired TPC Wire & Cable
- **2024:** Acquired PCTEL (wireless technology solutions)
- **Pending:** Trexon acquisition ($1B)
- **Pending:** CommScope CCS business acquisition ($10.5B)

### Innovation Leadership
- Birthplace of the MIL-DTL-38999 cylindrical connector (Amphenol Aerospace division)
- Invented the BNC connector (Bayonet Neill-Concelman)

1. **Diversification:** Operates across 10 of 11 market sectors, reducing cyclical exposure

5. **Global Presence:** Manufacturing in ~40 countries

8. **Scale Advantages:** Second-largest global market share
