**Stock Ticker:** BLK (NYSE)

## Main Products and Services

### 1. iShares ETFs

**Overview:**
- World's largest issuer of exchange-traded funds (ETFs)
- Acquired from Barclays Bank in 2009
- Managing $3.5 trillion in ETF investment vehicles (as of December 2023)

**Key Milestones:**
- **January 2024:** SEC approved BlackRock's spot Bitcoin ETF (IBIT)
  - First ETF to attract over $1 billion within one week
  - Reached $48 billion in assets by December 2024
- **March 2024:** Launched tokenized U.S. money market fund (BUIDL) on Ethereum
- **July 2024:** SEC approved iShares Ethereum Trust ETF (ETHA)
  - Gathered $2.25 billion in assets by December 2024
- **2024:** Became the largest issuer of crypto-backed ETFs

**Product Range:**
- Equity ETFs
- Fixed income ETFs
- Commodity ETFs
- Cryptocurrency ETFs (Bitcoin, Ethereum)
- Target allocation model portfolios

### 2. Aladdin Platform

**Overview:**
- Sophisticated institutional investment management software
- Provides unified platform for comprehensive portfolio view
- Features advanced risk analytics and generative AI tools
- Managed $21.6 trillion in assets as of 2020

**Key Features:**
- Portfolio management
- Risk management and analytics
- Trading execution and operations
- Compliance and regulatory reporting
- Generative AI integration

**Notable Clients:**
- Franklin Templeton
- Bank of Israel
- Microsoft's Treasury division
- Thousands of asset managers, insurers, and banks globally

**Integration:** iShares products are engineered by investment professionals with deep risk management expertise, powered by Aladdin.

### 3. Alternative Investment Solutions

**Recent Developments:**
- **2024:** Completed acquisition of Global Infrastructure Partners (GIP)
  - $3 billion in cash and 6.9 million shares of BlackRock common stock
  - Contingent consideration liability of $4.2 billion
- **2025:** Finalized acquisition of Preqin
  - Enhances integrated investment solutions across public and private markets
- **Announced:** Planned acquisition of HPS
  - Will scale and enhance private markets investment capabilities

## Company History and Key Milestones

### Founding (1988)
- Founded by eight partners led by Larry Fink
- Initially established under The Blackstone Group as Blackstone Financial Management
- Started with a $5 million credit line from Blackstone in exchange for 50% stake
- Original focus: Asset management from a risk-management perspective

### Early Growth (1989-1999)
- **1989:** Assets quadrupled to $2.7 billion within months
- **1991:** Reached $9 billion in AUM
- **1992:** Adopted the name "BlackRock"; grew to $17 billion AUM
- **1994:**
  - Split from Blackstone and became independent
  - Reached $53 billion in AUM
- **1998:** Larry Fink became Chairman
- **1999:** BlackRock went public

### Major Acquisitions (2000-2010)
- **2006:** Merged with Merrill Lynch Investment Managers
  - Doubled BlackRock's asset management portfolio
- **2009:** Acquired Barclays Global Investors (BGI)
  - Catapulted BlackRock to position of world's largest asset manager
  - Incorporated iShares, global leader in ETFs

### Modern Era (2020-Present)
- **2020:**
  - Federal Reserve partnered with BlackRock to purchase distressed securities during COVID-19 pandemic
  - CEO Larry Fink announced environmental sustainability as key goal
  - Committed to sell $500 million in coal-related assets
  - Created funds excluding companies profiting from fossil fuels
- **2024:**
  - Entered cryptocurrency ETF market with Bitcoin and Ethereum products
  - Completed GIP acquisition
  - Achieved record $641 billion in net inflows
  - Reached $11.6 trillion in AUM
- **2025:**
  - Finalized Preqin acquisition
  - Surpassed $13.5 trillion in AUM (Q3)
  - Announced HPS acquisition plans

## Main Competitors and Differentiation

### Market Position (2024)

**Equity ETF Market Share:**
- **Vanguard:** 30.1%
- **BlackRock:** 29.4%
- **State Street:** 14.8%
- Combined "Big Three": 74.3% of entire equity ETF market

### Assets Under Management Comparison

| Company | AUM | Market Position |
|---------|-----|----------------|
| **BlackRock** | $10.64T (2024) / $13.5T (Q3 2025) | #1 |
| **Vanguard** | $7T+ | #2 |
| **Fidelity** | $4.5T | #3 |
| **State Street** | $4T | #4 |

### Key Competitors and Their Differentiation

#### 1. Vanguard
**Differentiation:**
- Focus on long-term investments, diversification, and low costs
- Unique ownership structure: owned by member funds, which are owned by fund shareholders
- No outside investors - profits returned to shareholders
- Commitment to low-cost index funds and ETFs
- Strong retail investor base
- Unseated BlackRock's top spot in Broadridge's 2024 Best-Performing Fund Brand Ranking in the U.S.

#### 2. State Street
**Differentiation:**
- Specializes in investment management and servicing
- Expertise in custody, accounting, and administration services for institutional investors
- Leader in ETF space with SPDR product line
- Focus on institutional clients
- Strong in operational and administrative services

#### 3. Fidelity
**Differentiation:**
- Strong brand recognition among retail investors
- Extensive network of investor centers
- User-friendly online platform and mobile apps
- Zero-fee index funds offering
- Comprehensive financial planning services
- Strong 401(k) and retirement plan business

### BlackRock's Key Differentiators

#### 1. Aladdin Technology Platform
- Comprehensive risk management and investment platform
- Used globally by asset managers, insurers, and banks
- Generates additional technology services revenue stream
- Advanced data analytics and generative AI integration
- Unmatched in the industry

#### 2. Diversified Investment Strategies
- Active fund management alongside passive strategies
- Broad range of asset classes and investment vehicles
- Global reach and expertise across all markets
- Strong in both retail and institutional segments

#### 3. Innovation Leadership
- First major asset manager to launch Bitcoin ETF (2024)
- Pioneer in cryptocurrency and blockchain investments
- ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) investment focus
- Tokenized fund offerings (BUIDL on Ethereum)

#### 4. Scale and Global Presence
- World's largest asset manager
- Presence in 100+ countries
- Diversified revenue by geography
- Strong relationships with institutional clients and governments

#### 5. Private Markets Expansion
- Strategic acquisitions (GIP, Preqin, HPS)
- Building comprehensive public and private markets platform
- Alternative investment capabilities
- Infrastructure and real assets expertise

### Competitive Advantages
- **Technology:** Aladdin platform provides dual revenue stream and client lock-in
- **Scale:** Largest AUM enables economies of scale and market influence
- **Diversification:** Broad product range across asset classes and geographies
- **Innovation:** First-mover advantage in emerging areas (crypto ETFs, tokenization)
- **Brand:** Trusted advisor to institutions and governments worldwide
