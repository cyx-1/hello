# General Electric (GE) Company Research

## Company Overview

**Company Type:** Public Company
**Stock Ticker:** GE (NYSE)
**Headquarters:** Evendale, Ohio
**Founded:** 1892
**Current Market Cap:** $332.4 billion (as of December 29, 2025)
**Stock Price:** $315.14 (as of December 29, 2025)

General Electric has undergone significant transformation from a diversified conglomerate into a focused aerospace company. Following strategic spin-offs of its healthcare, finance, appliance, and energy businesses between 2016 and 2024, the company now operates primarily as **GE Aerospace**.

## Corporate Structure (2024-2025)

In April 2024, GE completed its final major restructuring, splitting into two independent public companies:

1. **GE Aerospace** (retains the GE ticker) - Commercial and defense aircraft engines
2. **GE Vernova** - Energy infrastructure (Power, Wind, Electrification)

The company also previously spun off **GE HealthCare** as an independent entity.

## Main Products and Business Segments

### GE Aerospace (Primary Business)

GE Aerospace is a world-leading provider of jet and turboprop engines, integrated systems, and services for commercial, military, business, and general aviation.

#### Key Product Lines:

**1. LEAP Engine Family (CFM International Joint Venture with Safran)**
- **Entry into Service:** 2016
- **Units in Operation:** 9,000+ engines
- **Applications:** Airbus A320neo (LEAP-1A), Boeing 737 MAX (LEAP-1B)
- **Performance:** 15% better fuel burn and CO₂ emissions vs CFM56
- **2025 Production:** ~2,000 LEAP engine shipments expected
- **Key Milestone:** LEAP-1A durability kit in 2025 more than doubles time-on-wing, matching CFM56 benchmark

**2. CFM56 (Legacy Engine)**
- **Status:** World's best-selling jet engine
- **Applications:** Airbus A320ceo, Boeing 737NG families
- **Current Status:** Still generating significant aftermarket revenue

**3. GE9X Engine**
- **Record:** Highest thrust rating at 134,300 pounds
- **Applications:** Boeing 777X
- **Efficiency:** 10% lower specific fuel consumption than predecessor GE90-115B
- **Market Position:** Most efficient wide-body engine in its thrust class

**4. GEnx Engine**
- Powers Boeing 787 Dreamliner and Boeing 747-8

**5. Defense & Propulsion Technologies**
- Military engines for various aircraft platforms
- Propulsion systems for defense applications

**6. Avionics and Digital Systems**
- Aircraft systems integration
- Digital solutions for aviation

### GE Vernova (Spun off in 2024)

Focus on energy infrastructure including:
- Power generation
- Wind energy
- Electrification systems
- Advanced research and consulting services

### GE HealthCare (Previously Spun Off)

- $18.3B business with 50,000 employees
- Medical imaging, ultrasound, patient care solutions
- Pharmaceutical diagnostics

## Main Competitors and Competitive Landscape

### Primary Competitors

#### 1. **Siemens**
- **Similarity:** Most similar competitor to historical GE conglomerate
- **Employees:** 303,000
- **Revenue:** ~$65 billion (last fiscal year)
- **Strengths:** Power generation, healthcare technologies, extensive technological capabilities
- **Recent Performance:** Net income of $3.73B (Q1 2023), gross profit of $7.73B
- **Competitive Focus:** Power generation, industrial automation, healthcare

#### 2. **Honeywell International**
- **Market Focus:** Aerospace and industrial automation
- **Strengths:** Avionics, propulsion systems, automation solutions
- **Aerospace Competition:** Provides advanced avionics and propulsion systems competing with GE Aviation
- **Market Context:** Global industrial automation market reached $211B in 2023
- **Differentiation:** Emphasis on digital transformation and smart building technologies

#### 3. **Other Major Competitors**
- **ABB** - Industrial automation and electrification
- **Mitsubishi Electric** - Diversified electronics and industrial systems
- **3M** - Industrial and aerospace materials
- **Emerson Electric** - Automation solutions
- **Toshiba** - Industrial systems and energy
- **Hitachi** - Industrial equipment and systems

### Competitive Differentiation

**GE's Key Advantages:**
- Dominant position in commercial aircraft engine market through CFM International joint venture
- Largest installed base of commercial engines globally
- Strong aftermarket services business with recurring revenue
- Advanced technology programs (RISE) positioning for future sustainability requirements
- 130+ years of engineering excellence and innovation

**How Competitors Differentiate:**
- **Siemens:** Broader industrial portfolio, stronger position in European markets, integrated digital solutions
- **Honeywell:** Focus on avionics and cockpit systems rather than engines, strong building automation business
- **Others:** Specialized focus in specific industrial segments rather than comprehensive aerospace coverage

## Industry Context

- **Market Leadership:** GE engines power the majority of commercial flights worldwide
- **Aftermarket Opportunity:** Large installed base creates significant long-term service revenue
- **Sustainability Focus:** Industry shift toward fuel efficiency driving LEAP and RISE development
- **Supply Chain:** Critical partner in global aerospace ecosystem with Boeing, Airbus dependencies
