# VeriSign, Inc. (VRSN)

## Company Overview

**Company Type:** Public Company
**Stock Ticker:** VRSN (NASDAQ)
**Headquarters:** Reston, Virginia, USA
**Founded:** April 12, 1995 by D. James Bidzos
**Employees:** 932
**Market Capitalization:** ~$26.7 billion
**Major Shareholder:** Berkshire Hathaway (13.7% ownership, ~$2.7 billion)

VeriSign, Inc. is a global provider of domain name registry services and critical internet infrastructure. The company operates the authoritative registry for .com, .net, .name, and .cc top-level domains and manages two of the world's 13 root servers that route internet traffic. VeriSign has provided uninterrupted domain name resolution services since 1997.

## Main Products and Services

### Core Registry Services

1. **.com and .net Domain Registry**
   - Sole authorized registry for .com, .net, .name, and .cc top-level domains
   - Manages relationships with ~3,000 ICANN-accredited registrars
   - Processes more than 100 million domain name transactions daily

2. **Root Zone Maintainer Services**
   - Operates two of the world's 13 internet root nameservers (A and J root servers)
   - Distributes root zone changes to all thirteen root servers
   - Only operator to manage more than one root server

3. **DNS Infrastructure**
   - Globally distributed registry operation across 60+ nations on six continents
   - Hundreds of technical sites ensuring security, stability, and resilience

### Value-Added Services

| Service | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Registry Lock** | Protects against domain hijacking for .com, .net, .cc, and .name domains |
| **ConsoliDate** | Allows selection of single renewal date for multiple domains |
| **NameStudio API** | Smart API providing domain name suggestions to drive registrations |
| **IDN Services** | Internationalized Domain Names supporting local-language domains |
| **EPP SDKs** | Tools for registrars to integrate with Shared Registry Service |
| **IDN SDK** | Data encoding tools for DNS registrations |

## Competitors and Differentiation

### Competitive Landscape

VeriSign operates in a unique position as a registry operator (managing the actual database of domain names) rather than a registrar (selling domains to end users).

| Category | Competitors | Market Context |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| Domain Registry | Afilias, Public Interest Registry | Direct registry competition |
| DNS Services | Cloudflare DNS (20.32%), GoDaddy DNS (32.63%), IONOS 1&1 DNS (7.88%) | DNS market share |
| Cybersecurity | Cloudflare, Akamai Technologies, Symantec | Security services |
| Domain Registrars | GoDaddy, Namecheap, Cloudflare Registrar | Downstream distribution |

### Key Competitors

#### Afilias
- **Positioning:** Direct competitor in registry services
- **Differentiation:** Operates various TLDs including .info, .org (via Public Interest Registry)
- **Strategy:** Focus on new gTLD opportunities

#### Cloudflare
- **Positioning:** Competes in DNS and security services
- **Differentiation:**
  - Offers domain registration at wholesale (registry) cost with no markup
  - Pioneer in DDoS protection and web application firewalls
  - Strong CDN and performance optimization
- **Strategy:** Freemium model with enterprise upsells

#### GoDaddy
- **Positioning:** World's largest domain registrar (81+ million domains)
- **Differentiation:**
  - One-stop shop for domains, hosting, and website building
  - Extensive marketing and brand recognition
  - User-friendly platform for small businesses
- **Strategy:** Bundle services, competitive initial pricing with higher renewal rates

### VeriSign's Competitive Advantages

1. **Regulatory Moat**
   - Exclusive .com and .net registry operator under ICANN agreement
   - 2024 contract renewal extends through 2030
   - High barriers to entry due to regulatory requirements

2. **Infrastructure Scale**
   - Decades of continuous investment in proprietary infrastructure
   - Global distribution across 60+ countries
   - 100% uptime record since 1997

3. **Critical Internet Role**
   - Operates 2 of 13 root servers (unique among operators)
   - Root Zone Maintainer for global DNS
   - Essential to internet stability

4. **Financial Stability**
   - Consistent revenue growth (4-7% annually)
   - High operating margins (~67-68%)
   - Strong cash flow generation
   - Berkshire Hathaway's confidence as largest shareholder

### Competitive Risks

- Regulatory changes to ICANN agreements
- Dependence on limited number of TLDs (.com generates majority of revenue)
- Declining .com/.net domain base
- Competition from new gTLDs and alternative naming systems
- Technological disruption (blockchain domains, decentralized DNS)

## Investment Highlights

### Strengths
- **Monopolistic Position:** Exclusive operator of .com and .net registries
- **Predictable Revenue:** Subscription-based model with high renewal rates
- **High Margins:** Operating margin consistently above 65%
- **Capital Light:** Minimal capex requirements relative to cash flow
- **Buffett Endorsement:** Berkshire Hathaway continues accumulating shares

### Considerations
- **Domain Base Decline:** .com/.net registrations down 3.3% from peak
- **Limited Growth Avenues:** Constrained by exclusive focus on registry services
- **Regulatory Dependence:** Business fundamentally tied to ICANN agreements
- **Price Ceiling:** Contract limits price increases to 7% per year in qualifying periods
