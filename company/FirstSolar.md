# First Solar Inc.

## Company Overview

**Type:** Public Company
**Stock Symbol:** FSLR (NASDAQ)
**Headquarters:** Tempe, Arizona, USA
**Founded:** 1990 (as Solar Cells, Inc.), rebranded as First Solar in 1999
**IPO:** 2006
**CEO:** Mark Widmar (since July 2016)
**Industry:** Solar Energy / Photovoltaic Manufacturing
**Market Cap:** ~$27.2 billion (as of November 2025)

First Solar, Inc. is a publicly traded American manufacturer of solar panels and the only U.S.-headquartered company among the world's largest solar manufacturers. The company specializes in thin-film cadmium telluride (CdTe) photovoltaic technology, differentiating itself from the crystalline silicon (c-Si) dominant market.

## Products and Technology

### Core Technology: Cadmium Telluride (CdTe) Thin Film

First Solar's proprietary CdTe thin-film technology offers several advantages:
- **Lower carbon footprint** than crystalline silicon panels
- **Better temperature coefficient** (-0.28% per degree C) - less energy loss in hot conditions
- **98% less semiconductor material** than traditional c-Si panels
- **Fastest energy payback time** in the industry
- **Lowest water footprint** in manufacturing

### Main Product Lines

#### Series 7
- **Latest generation** utility-scale solar module
- **Form factor:** Large format with innovative back rail mounting
- **Max power output:** Up to 545W
- **Target market:** U.S. utility-scale projects
- **Key feature:** 0.3% warranty-backed annual degradation rate
- **Manufacturing:** Ohio facilities (designed in California and Ohio)
- **Innovation:** First PV manufacturer to offer returnable/reusable packaging

#### Series 6
- **Established product line** for utility-scale installations
- **Form factor:** 1.23m x 2m optimized size
- **Max power output:** Up to 475W
- **Series 6 Plus Bifacial:** World's first bifacial thin film CdTe module
- **Manufacturing:** Ohio, India, and international facilities

## Competitors and Market Position

### Main Competitors

| Company | Headquarters | Technology | Key Differentiator |
|---------|--------------|------------|-------------------|
| **LONGi Green Energy** | China | Monocrystalline c-Si | Highest efficiency records, vertical integration |
| **JinkoSolar** | China | TOPCon c-Si | Global distribution, bifacial technology |
| **Trina Solar** | China | c-Si | R&D innovation, quality standards |
| **JA Solar** | China | c-Si | Large production capacity |
| **Canadian Solar** | Canada/China | c-Si | Global presence, project development |

### Market Concentration
- Top 4 manufacturers (JinkoSolar, LONGi, Trina, JA Solar) control ~60% of global shipments
- First Solar holds unique position as only major U.S.-headquartered manufacturer

### First Solar's Competitive Advantages

1. **Technology Differentiation**
   - CdTe thin-film vs. crystalline silicon market dominance
   - Superior hot weather performance
   - Lower environmental footprint

2. **Vertical Integration**
   - Module manufacturing
   - Project development capabilities
   - End-of-life recycling at all facilities

3. **Domestic Manufacturing**
   - Only major U.S.-headquartered solar manufacturer
   - Benefits from IRA domestic content incentives
   - Insulated from import tariffs on Chinese modules

4. **Sustainability Leadership**
   - Lowest carbon and water footprint
   - Responsible manufacturing practices
   - Recycling program for end-of-life modules

5. **Strong Balance Sheet**
   - $2 billion gross cash (Q3 2025)
   - Ability to invest during industry downturns
   - Self-funded expansion plans

### Competitive Challenges

1. **Scale:** Chinese competitors have larger production capacities
2. **Price Competition:** c-Si manufacturers compete aggressively on price
3. **Efficiency Race:** c-Si panels achieving efficiency gains
4. **Supply Chain:** Less integrated supply chain than vertically integrated c-Si players

### Differentiation Strategies

**First Solar's Approach:**
- Focus on utility-scale market (not residential/commercial)
- Emphasize total cost of ownership over module price
- Leverage U.S. manufacturing for policy benefits
- Invest in R&D for efficiency improvements
- Position as sustainable/responsible choice

**Competitor Approaches:**
- **LONGi:** Monocrystalline focus, efficiency leadership
- **JinkoSolar:** Distribution network, new technologies (n-type, TOPCon)
- **Trina Solar:** Innovation, quality, diverse product range
- **Canadian Solar:** Global project development, integrated solutions
