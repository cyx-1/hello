# Fidelity Investments

## Company Type: Private Company

Fidelity Investments is a privately-held company, meaning it is not publicly traded on any stock exchange. This is notable in an industry dominated by publicly traded companies.

### Ownership Structure

- **Johnson Family**: 49% ownership
- **Employees**: 51% ownership distributed among executives and employees
- **Leadership**: Abigail P. "Abby" Johnson serves as Chairman and CEO (since 2014), representing the third generation of Johnson family leadership
- **Corporate Entity**: FMR LLC (formerly Fidelity Management & Research)

Being privately held allows Fidelity more control over its long-term strategy and shields it from the pressure of quarterly earnings reports.

## Main Products and Services

### 1. Brokerage Services
- Commission-free online stock, ETF, and options trades
- Self-directed retail investing platform
- Access to over 30 international markets
- Cryptocurrency trading (Bitcoin, Ethereum) via Fidelity Crypto
- Precious metals trading (gold, silver, platinum, palladium)

### 2. Mutual Funds
- Three fund divisions: Equity (Boston), High-Income (Boston), Fixed-Income (Merrimack, NH)
- **Fidelity Contrafund**: $145 billion AUM - largest actively managed mutual fund in the U.S.
- Zero-fee mutual funds introduced in 2018

### 3. Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs)
- 66 ETFs in product lineup (as of 2024)
- **Bitcoin Spot ETF**: Launched January 2024 after SEC approval
- **Ethereum Spot ETF**: Launched July 2024 after SEC approval
- Fundamental Active ETF suite
- Fidelity Low Duration Bond ETF

### 4. Retirement Services
- 401(k) products (offered since 1982)
- 42 million plan participants

- Workplace investing and pension administration

### 5. Wealth Management & Advisory

  - 0.35% annual fee above $25,000
  - Unlimited 1-on-1 coaching calls
- Financial planning and investment advice
- Asset custody services

### 6. Digital Assets
- Fidelity Digital Asset Services (launched October 2018)
- Institutional cryptocurrency custody
- Cryptocurrency trading services

### 7. Other Services
- Donor-advised funds (first commercial offering launched 1991)
- Index funds
- Securities execution and clearance
- Life insurance

## Key Milestones and History

### Founding Era
- **1930**: Fidelity Fund incorporated in Massachusetts with Edward C. Johnson II as president
- **1943**: Edward C. Johnson II acquired the fund ($3 million AUM)
- **1946**: Founded Fidelity Management & Research Company (FMR Co.) as investment advisor

### Growth and Innovation (1950s-1970s)
- **1950s**: Introduced check-writing privileges for mutual fund investors (pioneering move)
- **1962**: Established Magellan Fund (later became world's largest mutual fund)
- **1969**: Formed Fidelity Service Company for in-house account servicing; Founded Fidelity International Limited (FIL)
- **1972**: Ned Johnson (2nd generation) succeeded father as president
- **1974**: Launched Fidelity Daily Income Trust (FIDIT) - first money market fund with check writing

### Expansion (1980s-1990s)
- **1982**: Began offering 401(k) products
- **1984**: Introduced computerized stock trading
- **1986**: Grew to 2,800 employees, 104 funds, $50B AUM, 2M+ customers
- **1991**: Launched first commercial donor-advised fund
- **1995**: Pioneer in offering online brokerage services

### Modern Era (2000s-Present)
- **2014**: Abigail Johnson (3rd generation) became President, CEO, and Chairman
- **2018**: Launched zero-fee mutual funds; Founded Fidelity Digital Asset Services
- **2022**: Began offering Bitcoin in 401(k) plans
- **2024**: Launched spot Bitcoin ETF (January) and Ethereum ETF (July)

## Main Competitors and Differentiation

### Top 3 Competitor Comparison

| Company | AUM (2024) | Founded | Ownership | Key Strengths |
|---------|------------|---------|-----------|---------------|
| **BlackRock** | $11.6 trillion | 1988 | Public | #1 globally, institutional focus, iShares ETFs |
| **Vanguard** | $10.1 trillion | 1975 | Private (client-owned) | Lowest-cost index funds, pioneered passive investing |
| **Fidelity** | $5.9 trillion | 1946 | Private (family & employee-owned) | Full-service platform, active trading tools, innovation |
| **Charles Schwab** | ~$8 trillion | 1971 | Public | Superior research tools (enhanced by TD Ameritrade acquisition), 400 branch locations |

### Fidelity's Key Differentiators

**1. Comprehensive Platform**
- Fidelity stands out as the most comprehensive platform, offering robust services for both active traders and passive investors
- "There isn't much that the company doesn't do" - serves beginners through advanced traders

**2. Superior Trading Platform**
- Best choice among the "Big 3" (Fidelity, Vanguard, Schwab) for active traders
- Advanced order execution and trading tools
- Superior educational resources

**3. Innovation Leadership**
- Early adopter of cryptocurrency services (Bitcoin 401(k) option, spot ETFs)
- Pioneer in digital asset custody (Fidelity Digital Asset Services)
- First to offer zero-fee mutual funds (2018)

**4. Robo-Advisor Advantage (Fidelity Go)**
- $0 minimum to start (vs. competitors' minimums)
- Free until $25,000 account balance
- Unlimited 1-on-1 coaching included
- Best robo-advisor offering among legacy brokers

**5. Private Ownership Benefit**
- Long-term strategic focus without quarterly earnings pressure
- Ability to invest in innovation without immediate profit requirements

### Vanguard's Differentiation
- **Lowest-cost leader**: Index funds with industry-lowest expense ratios
- **Unique ownership**: Client-owned structure (mutual company)
- **Passive investing pioneer**: Founded by John C. Bogle, inventor of index fund
- **Cult-like following** among passive investors
- **Weakness**: Customer service lags behind competitors

### Charles Schwab's Differentiation
- **Best-in-class research**: Enhanced by TD Ameritrade acquisition
- **17,000+ mutual funds**: 4,000 with no transaction fee
- **Largest branch network**: ~400 locations (2x Fidelity's footprint)
- **International access**: Trading in 30 international markets (most among competitors)

- Greater control over long-term strategy
