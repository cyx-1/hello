# Lockheed Martin Corporation

**Ticker:** NYSE: LMT
**Headquarters:** Bethesda, Maryland, USA
**Industry:** Aerospace & Defense
**Founded:** 1995 (merger of Lockheed Corporation and Martin Marietta)
**Employees:** ~122,000

## Company Overview

Lockheed Martin Corporation is the world's largest defense contractor, specializing in aerospace, defense, arms, information security, and technology. The company operates through four business segments: Aeronautics, Missiles and Fire Control, Rotary and Mission Systems, and Space.

## Main Products and Key Milestones

### F-35 Lightning II (26% of Total Revenue)

The F-35 is a family of single-engine, supersonic, stealth multirole strike fighters and Lockheed Martin's largest program.

**Variants:**
- **F-35A**: Conventional takeoff and landing (CTOL) - $82.5M per unit
- **F-35B**: Short takeoff/vertical landing (STOVL) - $109M per unit
- **F-35C**: Carrier-based variant - $102.1M per unit

**Capabilities:**
- 5th generation stealth fighter with advanced avionics and sensor fusion
- Internal weapons capacity up to 5,700 lbs; external capacity over 18,000 lbs
- Can carry Joint Air to Surface Standoff Missile and Long Range Anti-Ship Missile
- CF-35 'sidekick' system allows 6 AIM-120D missiles internally

**U.S. Procurement Plans:**
- USAF: 1,763 F-35As
- USMC: 353 F-35Bs and 67 F-35Cs
- USN: 273 F-35Cs

**Key Milestones:**
- 2024: Surpassed 1,000 total F-35 deliveries
- 2024: Delivered 110 F-35s, meeting annual commitment
- 2024: F-35 deliveries resumed in July with Tech Refresh 3 software approval
- 2025: Graduated 3,000th pilot through F-35 Training System
- 2025: Completed delivery of Norway's F-35 program of record
- NAVAIR awarded $97.3M contract to integrate AGM-88 HARM missile on F-35

### Missiles and Fire Control Systems

**HIMARS (High Mobility Artillery Rocket System)**
- Key weapon system in modern conflicts
- Successfully delivered first launchers to Estonia in 2024
- Increased production across U.S. facilities

**Precision Strike Missile (PrSM)**
- First Soldier-led Limited User Test completed at White Sands Missile Range
- Approaching Initial Operating Capability in 2025

**Mako Hypersonic Missile**
- Compact hypersonic weapon traveling at more than 5x speed of sound
- Sized to fit F-35A/C internal bays
- Proposed UK production facility announced at Farnborough 2024

**PAC-3 Missile Segment Enhancement (MSE)**
- Advanced air defense interceptor
- Increased production capacity

**Javelin Anti-Tank Missile**
- Portable fire-and-forget anti-tank system
- Joint venture with RTX Corporation

**GMLRS (Guided Multiple Launch Rocket System)**
- Precision munitions for MLRS/HIMARS platforms

### Next Generation Interceptor (NGI)

Selected by U.S. Missile Defense Agency to protect homeland against ballistic missile threats from rogue nations.

### Space Systems

**Next-Gen OPIR GEO Satellite**
- December 2024: Fully assembled and powered on first satellite
- Part of Space Force's multi-orbit missile warning and tracking architecture

**Global Mobile Artillery Rocket System (GMARS)**
- Unveiled at Eurosatory 2024 in partnership with Rheinmetall

### Competitor Analysis

#### RTX Corporation (Raytheon Technologies)
- **Formed:** 2020 merger of Raytheon Company and United Technologies Corporation
- **Key Competition:** Patriot missile defense vs. Lockheed's THAAD system
- **Differentiation:** Specializes in sensors and smart weapons with AI/ML integration
- **Future Focus:** Directed energy weapons, autonomous systems, cybersecurity

#### Northrop Grumman
- **Key Competition:** B-21 Raider stealth bomber competes for defense dollars with F-35
- **Differentiation:** Leader in stealth technology and high-altitude long-endurance (HALE) drones
- **Products:** Global Hawk, Triton drones compete with Lockheed's U-2 aircraft
- **Strategic Move:** 2018 acquisition of Orbital ATK boosted space capabilities
- **Future Focus:** Directed energy weapons, quantum computing, next-gen air dominance

#### Boeing
- **Key Competition:** Military aircraft development, missile defense, space exploration
- **Differentiation:** Dual commercial/defense portfolio provides diverse revenue streams
- **Strategic Focus:** Sustainable aviation, electric and hydrogen-powered aircraft
- **Future Focus:** Autonomous systems, AI, sustainable aviation technologies

#### General Dynamics
- **Differentiation:** Specializes in ground combat vehicles, submarines, and IT services
- **Products:** Abrams tank, Virginia-class submarines, Gulfstream business jets
- **Less Direct Competition:** Different primary focus areas than Lockheed Martin

### Lockheed Martin Competitive Advantages

1. **Market Leadership:** Largest defense contractor globally by revenue
2. **F-35 Program:** Dominant position in 5th generation fighter market
3. **Government Relationships:** 73% revenue from U.S. Government contracts
4. **Technological Expertise:** Leading capabilities in stealth, hypersonics, and space
5. **Backlog:** Record $176 billion backlog provides revenue visibility
6. **Strategic Partnerships:** Collaborations with government agencies and research institutions
7. **International Presence:** Strong relationships with allied nations

### Market Dynamics

The defense industry is characterized by:
- Long procurement cycles and multi-year contracts
- High barriers to entry due to technical complexity
- Significant R&D investment requirements
- Geopolitical factors driving demand
- Increasing focus on hypersonics, AI/ML, autonomous systems, and space
