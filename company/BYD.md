# BYD Company Limited

## Company Overview

**Status:** Public Company
**Founded:** February 1995
**Headquarters:** Shenzhen, China
**Stock Tickers:**
- SZSE: 002594 (Class A shares)
- HKEX: 1211 (H shares)
- OTC: BYDDF (H shares)
- OTC: BYDDY (ADR)

**Market Cap:** $113.88 billion (as of December 29, 2025)

BYD (Build Your Dreams) evolved from a battery manufacturer founded in 1995 to a global leader in new energy vehicles (NEVs). The company listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange in 2002 and entered the automotive industry in 2003. On November 18, 2024, BYD became the world's first automaker to produce 10 million NEVs, achieving cumulative production of 15 million units by December 2025—exceeding the combined lifetime electric-vehicle production of Tesla (8.1 million) and Volkswagen Group (under 3 million).

## Main Products & Technologies

### Electric Vehicles

BYD produces electric vehicles at affordable prices and manufactures electric buses operating in over 70 countries, dominating the world's plug-in hybrid market. The company sold 4.27 million vehicles in 2024.

**Vehicle Series:**
- **Dynasty Series:** Including Han EV/DM-i, Tang L, and flagship sedan models
- **Ocean Series:** Featuring concept car "Ocean-S"
- **Yangwang:** Ultra-luxury brand featuring U8 model
- **Fangcheng Leopard:** Performance-oriented brand
- **Tengshi (Denza):** Premium electric vehicle brand
- **Commercial Vehicles:** T5 light-duty truck series, electric buses

### Battery Technology

#### Blade Battery (2020)

BYD's signature lithium iron phosphate (LFP) battery launched in 2020, designed and manufactured by FinDreams Battery subsidiary.

**Key Features:**
- Energy density: 150 Wh/kg
- Space utilization increased by over 50% vs. conventional LFP batteries
- Superior safety: In nail penetration tests, surface temperature only reached 30-60°C (vs. 500°C+ for ternary lithium batteries)
- First deployed: Han EV (June 2020) with 605 km range

#### Blade Battery 2.0 (2025)

Second-generation Blade Battery launching mid-2025 in Europe:
- Energy density: Up to 210 Wh/kg (40% improvement)
- 8C ultra-fast charging: Full charge in ~7.5 minutes
- Megawatt (MW) Flash Charging: 1,000 kW power delivery

#### Future: Solid-State Battery (Target 2030)

- Tested in BYD vehicles in China
- Range: Up to 1,500 km NEDC
- Mass production expected by 2030

### Competitive Differentiation

#### BYD's Strategy
- **Product Range:** Wide portfolio covering battery-only EVs and plug-in hybrids
- **Price Positioning:** Competitive pricing across broad price bands, particularly strong in non-Western markets
- **Technology:** Vertical integration with proprietary battery technology (LFP Blade Battery)
- **Market Focus:** Targeting first-time buyers in markets with charging infrastructure challenges
- **Geographic Expansion:** Aggressive international growth, outselling Tesla in Europe in multiple months

#### Tesla's Strategy
- **Product Focus:** Battery-only models with short lineup
- **Technology:** Software-first approach with over-the-air updates
- **Market Challenges:** U.S. market share dropped to 38% in August 2025 (down from ~80% in 2020)
- **Regional Performance:** Volumes down 49% YoY in Europe (April)

#### Volkswagen's Strategy
- **Legacy Transformation:** Traditional automaker transitioning to EVs
- **Market Share:** 7% BEV portfolio globally
- **Cumulative Production:** Under 3 million EVs (vs. BYD's 15 million)

### Recent Milestones (Europe)
In April 2025, BYD outsold Tesla in Europe for the first time, with registrations surging 359% year-over-year while Tesla dropped 49%.
