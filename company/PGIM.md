# PGIM (Prudential Global Investment Management)

## Company Structure

**Type:** Public (Subsidiary)

PGIM is an indirect, wholly-owned subsidiary of Prudential Financial, Inc. (NYSE: PRU), a publicly traded company on the New York Stock Exchange. While PGIM itself is not separately publicly traded, it operates under the public company umbrella. PGIM, Inc. is organized as a Delaware limited liability company and is registered as an investment adviser with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC).

**Important Note:** Prudential Financial, Inc. of the United States is not affiliated with Prudential plc (incorporated in the United Kingdom) or Prudential Assurance Company (a subsidiary of M&G plc, incorporated in the United Kingdom).

## Main Products and Services

### Business Units and Capabilities

PGIM operates through a multi-affiliate/multi-boutique structure, with each unit maintaining specialized expertise:

#### 1. **PGIM Fixed Income**
- Global asset manager offering active solutions across all fixed income markets
- As of June 2025: Merged with Private Capital to create ~$1 trillion combined unit
- Approximately $862B AUM (pre-merger)

#### 2. **PGIM Real Estate**
- **AUM:** $132.5B (net), $47.3B AUA (as of December 31, 2024)
- **Global Presence:** 32 offices worldwide
- **Services:** Real estate investment and financing across global markets
- **Note:** Merged debt and equity businesses in 2020

#### 3. **PGIM Private Capital**
- **Focus:** Private debt for public and private companies, middle-market lending
- **2024 H1 Activity:** $7.5B in senior debt and junior capital to 130+ companies globally
- Approximately $110B AUM (before merger with Fixed Income)
- **Major Acquisition:** Acquired majority interest in Deerpath Capital (2023) - $5B+ AUM in lower middle-market private credit

#### 4. **PGIM Private Alternatives**
- **AUM:** $324B (gross) in private alternatives strategies
- **Asset Classes:** Private credit, real estate, agriculture, sustainable investing, infrastructure, private equity

#### 5. **PGIM Quantitative Solutions (PGIM Quant)**
- **Founded:** 1975 (originally as Quantitative Management Associates - QMA)
- **Specialty:** Systematic investing, quantitative strategies
- **Note:** Integrating with Multi-Asset Solutions team as of 2025

#### 6. **PGIM Multi-Asset Solutions**
- Total portfolio approach to asset allocation
- Integrating with quantitative solutions capabilities under leadership of Phil Waldeck

#### 7. **PGIM Investments (Retail)**
- Direct indexing platform via PGIM Custom Harvest (acquired Green Harvest Asset Management in 2021)
- Separately managed accounts (SMA) for high-net-worth investors

### Competitive Differentiation Strategy

#### 1. **Multi-Affiliate/Multi-Boutique Model**
- Autonomous specialized units with deep asset-class expertise
- Each affiliate maintains distinct governance and investment processes
- Enables focused specialization while providing scale benefits

#### 2. **150-Year Insurance Heritage**
- Vertical integration: $330B in private markets + $1T+ in public markets
- Self-reinforcing asset-liability matching capabilities
- Conservative underwriting approach with disciplined credit risk management
- Successfully navigated 30+ market cycles

#### 3. **Global Distribution Network**
- Nearly 250 global distribution professionals
- Structured training protocols and flexible engagement models
- Clear escalation rules providing scalability for wealth platforms

#### 4. **Credit Discipline**
- Prioritizes covenant protection and disciplined entry leverage
- Active credit risk management over maximizing scale
- Differentiated from pure-play asset managers and recently assembled multi-strategy firms

#### 5. **Global Scale with Local Presence**
- 42+ offices worldwide facilitating access to differentiated deal flow
- Co-investment opportunities through local market knowledge
- Combined public and private markets expertise

### How Competitors Differentiate

- **BlackRock:** Technology platform (Aladdin), passive/active integration, scale advantage
- **Vanguard:** Low-cost index funds, client-owned structure, passive investing focus
- **Fidelity:** Integrated wealth management, direct retail distribution, technology innovation
- **State Street:** Institutional focus, ETF leadership (SPDR), custodial banking integration
- **PIMCO:** Fixed income specialization, active management expertise
- **J.P. Morgan/Goldman Sachs:** Full-service investment banking integration, alternative investments

PGIM differentiates through its insurance-backed stability, multi-boutique specialist model, and integrated public-private markets platform rather than competing on pure scale or low-cost indexing.
