# Illinois Tool Works Inc.

**Stock Symbol:** NYSE: ITW
**Founded:** 1912
**Headquarters:** Glenview, Illinois, USA

## Overview

Illinois Tool Works Inc. (ITW) is a diversified manufacturer of industrial products and equipment operating through seven business segments. The company serves customers in 51 countries with approximately 44,000 employees. ITW is renowned for its decentralized business structure and the application of the "80/20 principle" across all operations—focusing resources on the 20% of customers who generate 80% of profits. The company emphasizes operational excellence, achieving industry-leading margins through continuous improvement and disciplined capital allocation.

## Key Milestones

- **1912:** Byron L. Smith founds Illinois Tool Works in Chicago with initial investment of $40,000
- **1923:** Acquires Shakeproof Screw and Nut Lock Company (innovative locking washers for automotive)
- **1948:** Goes public, begins trading on NYSE
- **1955:** International expansion begins (Canada, Mexico)
- **1959:** Establishes Hi-Cone division (beverage packaging systems)
- **1982:** John D. Nichols becomes CEO, accelerates acquisition strategy
- **1985:** Adopts **80/20 principle** as core business philosophy
  - Focus on 20% of customers driving 80% of profits
  - Divest or streamline bottom 20% of underperforming products/customers
- **1986:** Acquires Signode Industries for $524M (largest acquisition to date, packaging systems)
  - Doubled ITW's revenue overnight
- **1990s:** Aggressive acquisition spree—27 acquisitions in 1980s, 50+ in 1990s
- **1996:** Reaches 365 separate operating divisions (peak decentralization)
- **2006:** Acquires Click Commerce (industrial software)
- **2012:** Reaches 800+ business units after acquiring 600+ companies (1985-2012)
- **2012-2018:** **"Enterprise Strategy" transformation:**
  - Consolidated 800+ units into 84 divisions
  - Divested $3B of low-margin businesses
  - Focused on seven core segments
- **2017:** Divests decorative surfaces business (Wilsonart retained as equity stake)
- **2020:** COVID-19 pandemic: Pivoted to produce PPE, hand sanitizer
- **2024:** Sells remaining Wilsonart equity stake for $1.0B; achieves record 26.8% operating margin
- **2024:** 62 consecutive years of dividend increases (Dividend Aristocrat status)

### ITW's Competitive Advantages

1. **80/20 Business Process (Core Differentiator):**
   - **Customer segmentation:** Ruthlessly focus on top 20% of customers generating 80% of gross profit
   - **Product simplification:** Eliminate SKU proliferation (SKU reduction of 40% since 2012)
   - **Example:** Automotive OEM rationalized from 5,000 SKUs to 3,000 (2015-2020), operating margin +500 bps
   - **Capital discipline:** ITW divested $3B of businesses 2012-2018 (bottom 20% performers)

2. **Decentralized Operating Model:**
   - **84 autonomous divisions** (down from 800+ in 2012 after consolidation)
   - **Division P&L ownership:** General managers control pricing, product development, hiring (no centralized mandate)
   - **Speed to market:** Divisions launch products in 6-12 months vs. competitors' 18-24 months (centralized bureaucracy)
   - **Retention:** Division GM tenure averages 12 years (long-term accountability)

3. **Consumables and Recurring Revenue:**
   - **50%+ of revenue** from consumables/aftermarket (2024):
     - Welding: 60% filler metals, electrodes
     - Paslode: 40% fuel cells
     - Food Equipment: 35% parts/service for Hobart installed base
     - Hi-Cone: 100% consumable (multi-pack rings replaced every beverage cycle)
   - **Installed base lock-in:** 2M+ Paslode nailers, 1M+ Hobart dishwashers → captive aftermarket

4. **Industry-Leading Operating Margins:**
   - **26.8% operating margin (2024):** Best-in-class among diversified industrials
     - vs. 3M 18.5%, Stanley 8.5%, Fortive 21.3%, Honeywell 19.2%
   - **Segment margins:** Six of seven segments >25% operating margin (2024)
   - **Margin expansion:** +300 bps (2022-2024) driven by pricing, 80/20 execution, restructuring

5. **Enterprise Initiatives (Organic Growth Investments):**
   - **$700M+ invested 2022-2024:**
     - **Marketing/sales:** Embedded sales engineers at top 100 customers
     - **Innovation:** Paslode cordless framing nailer (Gen 3, 2023), Hobart connected dishwasher (IoT monitoring)
     - **Digital:** E-commerce platform for Polymers & Fluids (direct-to-contractor sales)
   - **Organic growth acceleration:** -0.7% (2024) → targeting +3-4% (2025+)

6. **Capital Allocation Discipline:**
   - **100%+ free cash flow returned to shareholders (2024):**
     - Dividends: $1.2B (62 consecutive years of increases)
     - Buybacks: $2.6B (8.4% shares repurchased)
   - **M&A discipline:** ITW 3x ROIC hurdle on acquisitions (2 acquisitions in 2024, tuck-ins <$100M)
   - **No mega-deals:** Largest acquisition since 2012 was $220M (Finite Elemente, Test & Measurement, 2021)
