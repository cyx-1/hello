# Corning Incorporated

## Company Overview

**Ticker Symbol:** GLW (NYSE)
**Founded:** 1851
**Headquarters:** Corning, New York, USA
**Employees:** 56,300 (2024)
**Industry:** Specialty Glass, Ceramics, and Optical Physics

Corning Incorporated is an American multinational technology company specializing in glass, ceramics, and related materials and technologies including advanced optics, primarily for industrial and scientific applications. The company went public on December 31, 1981.

For more than 170 years, Corning has combined its unparalleled expertise in glass science, ceramics science, and optical physics with deep manufacturing and engineering capabilities to develop life-changing innovations. Notable innovations include the first glass bulbs for Thomas Edison's electric light, the first low-loss optical fiber, cellular substrates for catalytic converters, and damage-resistant cover glass for mobile devices.

## Main Products and Key Milestones

### Core Product Lines

#### 1. Gorilla Glass
A high-strength alkali-aluminosilicate thin sheet glass used as protective cover glass for consumer electronics.

**Version Timeline:**
- **2007-2008:** Original Gorilla Glass (first used on iPhone)
- **January 2012:** Gorilla Glass 2 (20% thinner)
- **January 2013:** Gorilla Glass 3 (3x more scratch-resistant)
- **2016:** Gorilla Glass 5 (enhanced durability)
- **Late 2018:** Gorilla Glass 6 (multiple drops from 1.6m)
- **July 2020:** Gorilla Glass Victus (2m drop protection, 2x scratch resistance)
- **November 2022:** Gorilla Glass Victus 2 (improved concrete drop performance)
- **January 2024:** Gorilla Armor (75% reflection reduction)
- **2024:** Gorilla Glass 7i (budget/mid-range devices)
- **2025:** Gorilla Glass Ceramic (transparent, strengtheable glass ceramic)

**Milestone:** Used on more than 8 billion devices by over 45 major global brands.

#### 2. Optical Fiber and Cable
World's leading manufacturer of optical fiber for telecommunications.

**Key Innovations:**
- Pioneered low-loss optical fiber
- **2007:** ClearCurve fiber with nanostructure technology for FTTX installations
- Operates world's largest and lowest-cost optical-fiber production facility in Concord, NC

#### 3. Ceramic Substrates and Filters
Major manufacturer for emissions control in automotive applications (catalytic converters).

#### 4. Display Glass
Leading manufacturer of glass substrates for LCD televisions, computer monitors, and laptops.

#### 5. Life Sciences Products
Laboratory products including plastic vessels, liquid handling plastics, specialty surfaces, cell culture media, and glassware under brands: Corning, Pyrex, Falcon, and Axygen.

### Recent Strategic Developments (2024-2025)

- **Broadcom Partnership:** Collaboration to develop co-packaged optics (CPO) infrastructure for AI data centers
- **AT&T Agreement:** Multi-year deal worth over $1 billion to enhance fiber network capabilities
- **Springboard Plan:** Initiative to add $3B+ in annualized sales and achieve 20% operating margin by end of 2026

### Competitor Profiles

#### AGC Inc. (Japan)
- World's largest glass manufacturing company
- Core member of Mitsubishi group
- Specializes in automotive glazing, architectural glass, display substrates
- Operations in 30+ countries

#### Schott AG (Germany)
- 12.3% share of glass ceramics market
- German engineering legacy
- Known for CERAN cooktop panels
- Specializes in specialty glass and glass-ceramics

#### Nippon Sheet Glass Co., Ltd. (Japan)
- NSG Group with Pilkington brand heritage
- Advanced automotive glazing
- Self-cleaning glass technologies

### Corning's Key Differentiators

1. **Proprietary Technologies**
   - Fusion manufacturing process (patented)
   - Specialized glass compositions
   - Ion-exchange strengthening processes

2. **Brand Equity**
   - Strong recognition of Gorilla Glass brand
   - 170+ years of materials science expertise
   - Trusted supplier to major OEMs

3. **Scale and Cost Leadership**
   - World's largest optical fiber production facility
   - Economies of scale in manufacturing
   - Integrated supply chain

4. **Innovation Culture**
   - Continuous R&D investment ($1.09B in 2024)
   - Atomic-scale modeling capabilities
   - Regular product generation improvements

5. **Strategic Partnerships**
   - Deep relationships with Apple, Samsung, and other tech leaders
   - Collaboration with AT&T, Broadcom for next-gen infrastructure
   - Long-term supply agreements
