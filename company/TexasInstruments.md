# Texas Instruments Incorporated (TXN)

## Company Overview

**Type:** Public Company
**Stock Exchange:** NASDAQ: TXN
**Headquarters:** Dallas, Texas, USA
**Founded:** 1930 (as Geophysical Service Inc.)
**Renamed:** 1951 (to Texas Instruments)
**Market Cap:** $144.77 billion (November 2025)
**Employees:** ~34,000
**Industry:** Semiconductors

Texas Instruments Incorporated (TI) is an American multinational semiconductor company that designs, manufactures, and sells semiconductors to electronics designers and manufacturers globally. The company is the world's largest manufacturer of analog semiconductors, holding approximately 19% market share in that segment.

### Analog Segment
- **Products:** Power management, signal chain
- **Applications:** Condition, amplify, and convert real-world signals (sound, temperature, pressure, images)
- **Power management:** Converting, distributing, storing, discharging, isolating, and measuring electrical energy

### Embedded Processing Segment
- **Products:** Microcontrollers, digital signal processors (DSPs), applications processors, wireless connectivity, radar
- **Key Features:** Self-contained systems with processor core, memory, and peripherals

### Other Segment
- **Products:** DLP (Digital Light Processing) products, calculators, custom ASICs (application-specific integrated circuits)

## Competitive Landscape

### Main Competitors

| Company | Market Share (Analog) | Key Strengths |
|---------|----------------------|---------------|
| **Texas Instruments** | 19% | Manufacturing control, product breadth, in-house production |
| **Analog Devices** | 13% | High-performance analog, precision, industrial focus |
| **NXP Semiconductors** | ~8% | Automotive ADAS, secure connectivity |
| **Infineon** | ~7% | Power semiconductors, automotive |
| **STMicroelectronics** | ~6% | Broad portfolio, automotive |

### Texas Instruments' Competitive Advantages

1. **In-House Manufacturing**
   - Most competitors outsource production; TI controls ~80% internally
   - 300mm wafer fabs provide 40% cost advantage over 200mm

2. **Product Breadth**
   - ~80,000 products serving ~100,000 customers
   - One-stop shop for hardware manufacturers

3. **Long-Lived Products**
   - Many products have 10+ year lifecycles
   - Reduces design-in churn and customer acquisition costs

4. **R&D Investment**
   - $1.7B+ annually (2024)
   - Focus on analog and embedded processing

5. **Capital Allocation**
   - Highest return on invested capital in semiconductor industry
   - Consistent shareholder returns through dividends and buybacks

6. **Geographic Reach**
   - Global distribution with strong presence in all major markets
   - Strategic capacity in U.S. for supply chain resilience

### Competitive Dynamics by Market

**Industrial:**
- TI and Analog Devices compete directly
- TI has broader product coverage
- ADI has higher precision for specialized applications

**Automotive:**
- NXP leads in ADAS and in-vehicle networking
- TI has broader analog portfolio
- Competition intensifying in 48V and 800V systems

**Personal Electronics:**
- Highly price-competitive
- TI leverages manufacturing scale
- Focus shifting to industrial/automotive (higher margins)

## Summary

Texas Instruments remains the dominant player in analog semiconductors, with a 70-year history of innovation from the integrated circuit to modern embedded processing. Despite cyclical headwinds in 2023-2024 that reduced revenue by 22% from peak levels, the company maintains strong competitive advantages in manufacturing, product breadth, and customer relationships. Strategic investments of $60B+ in U.S. manufacturing capacity position TI for long-term growth in industrial automation and automotive electrification markets.
