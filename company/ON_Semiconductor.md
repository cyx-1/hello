## Company History

ON Semiconductor was founded in 1999 when Motorola spun off its Semiconductor Components Group as an independent company. TPG Capital led the acquisition for approximately $1.6 billion. The company went public on NASDAQ in March 2000.

### Key Historical Milestones

- **1999**: Spinoff from Motorola as ON Semiconductor
- **2000**: IPO on NASDAQ; acquired Cherry Semiconductor Corp. for $250M
- **2008**: Acquired AMI Semiconductor for $915M
- **2016**: Acquired Fairchild Semiconductor for $2.4B
- **2020**: Hassane El-Khoury appointed as CEO (previously CEO of Cypress Semiconductor)
- **2021**: Rebranded to "onsemi"
- **2023**: South Korea SiC wafer factory expansion completed
- **2025**: Acquired Qorvo's SiC JFET business for $115M

## Main Products and Technologies

### Silicon Carbide (SiC) - EliteSiC Portfolio

**Products**:
- M3e SiC MOSFETs: Industry's lowest specific on-resistance
- 1200V M3S EliteSiC MOSFETs: Up to 20% power loss reduction
- SiC Diodes: Higher efficiency, temperature resistance
- Si/SiC Hybrid Modules

**Key Milestones**:
- 2023: SiC revenue exceeded $800M (4x YoY growth)
- 2023: Rose to #2 globally in SiC power device market
- 2023: South Korea SiC fab expansion completed
- 2025: Transitioning to 8-inch SiC production
- 2025: Acquired Qorvo's SiC JFET business ($115M)

**Applications**: EV traction inverters, DC-DC converters, off-board chargers, AI data center power supplies

### Image Sensors

**Market Position**:
- #1 in automotive CIS with 46% market share
- 68% share in ADAS CIS market

**Products**:
- Hyperlux sensors for ADAS and autonomous driving
- Silicon Photomultipliers (SiPM)
- Single Photon Avalanche Diodes (SPAD)
- Thermal management sensors

**Applications**: Advanced driver assistance, autonomous vehicles, industrial vision, medical imaging

### Power Management

- MOSFETs (various voltages)
- IGBTs and IGBT modules
- Intelligent Power Modules (IPMs)
- Power factor correction
- Motor drivers

### Discrete & Analog

- Diodes and rectifiers
- Transistors
- Protection devices
- Analog signal chain
- Gate drivers

### Main Competitors

| Company | Strengths | 2023 Auto Semi Market Share |

| **Infineon Technologies** | Leading in power semiconductors; 17.6% power market share; Strong in SiC | 13.7% |
| **NXP Semiconductors** | #1 in automotive MCUs; Strong in secure connectivity | 10.8% |
| **STMicroelectronics** | #1 in SiC (32.6% share); Diversified portfolio | 10.2% |
| **Texas Instruments** | Analog leader; Broad portfolio | 8.5% |
| **Renesas Electronics** | Strong in automotive MCUs | 6.7% |
| **onsemi** | #2 in SiC; #1 in automotive image sensors | ~6% |

### onsemi's Competitive Differentiation

1. **Vertical Integration in SiC**: End-to-end control from ingot growth to device fabrication
2. **Image Sensor Leadership**: Dominant position in ADAS with 68% market share
3. **Automotive Focus**: 80% revenue from automotive and industrial
4. **Long-Term Supply Agreements (LTSAs)**: Secured customer commitments for capacity
5. **Manufacturing Scale**: Global manufacturing with 80,000+ different parts
6. **Technology Breadth**: Power + Sensing combination unique in industry

### Industry Challenges

- Cyclical semiconductor demand
- EV adoption pace variability
- China market softness (25%+ sequential auto decline expected Q1 2025)
- Competition from vertically integrated competitors (Infineon, STMicro)
