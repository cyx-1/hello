KLA Corporation is an American company that makes wafer fab equipment, supplying process control and yield management systems for the semiconductor industry. The company's products cover all phases of wafer, reticle, integrated circuit (IC), and packaging production.

## Main Products & Key Milestones

### Core Product Categories

1. **Wafer Inspection Systems**
   - Surfscan SP7XP (2020) - Unpatterned wafer defect inspection with machine learning
   - Surfscan SP7 (2018) - Bare wafer inspection for 7nm logic nodes

2. **E-Beam Review Systems**
   - eDR7380 - Electron-beam inspection for sub-10nm processes

3. **Metrology Systems**
   - PWG5 (2020) - Patterned wafer geometry system
   - Film thickness, stress, and layer alignment measurement

4. **Optical Review Systems**
   - Micro-SR - High resolution defect review and metrology

5. **Mask/Reticle Inspection**
   - PUMA 9980 - Patterning and mask inspection

### KLA's Competitive Differentiation

1. **Market Dominance:** ~56% market share in process control; 4x larger than nearest competitor

2. **Specialization:** Sole focus on inspection and metrology vs. competitors with broader portfolios

3. **Highest Margins:**
   - Operating margin: 37.58% (highest among peers)
   - Gross margin: 59.75% (highest among peers)

4. **AI Leadership:**
   - Deep learning defect classification
   - AI-powered yield management software (Klarity)

5. **Data Network Effects:**
   - 25,000+ installed tool base creates proprietary data advantage
   - 3,000+ patents in nanoscale measurement

6. **High Switching Costs:**
   - Deep integration with fab ecosystems
   - Multi-year replacement cycles

7. **Complementary Role:**
   - Essential partner to other equipment providers
   - Not direct competitor but integral to manufacturing ecosystem

8. **Advanced Packaging Growth:**
   - Revenue from advanced packaging ~$500M in CY2024
   - Expected to reach $925M+ in 2025 (~70% growth)

9. **AI/HPC Enabler:**
   - Critical for NVIDIA, AMD, Intel AI chip manufacturing

## Company History Summary

KLA Corporation traces its roots to 1975 when Ken Levy and Bob Anderson founded KLA Instruments, focusing on photomask detection. The company merged with Tencor Instruments in 1997 in a $1.3 billion deal, creating KLA-Tencor and becoming the dominant process control player in semiconductors. The company acquired Orbotech for $3.4 billion in 2019, expanding into PCB, flat panel displays, and packaging markets.
