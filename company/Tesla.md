# Tesla, Inc. - Company Research

## Company Overview
Tesla, Inc. is a **public company** (NASDAQ: TSLA) and the world's leading electric vehicle manufacturer and clean energy company. Founded in 2003 and headquartered in Austin, Texas, Tesla designs, develops, manufactures, and sells electric vehicles, energy storage systems, and solar energy products.

## Product Lineup & Key Milestones

### Current Vehicle Lineup (2024-2025)

#### Core "S3XY" Models:
1. **Model S** (Sedan)
   - Last major refresh: 2021 (new interior and updated model lineup)
   - No significant changes expected for 2025
   - Premium luxury sedan segment

2. **Model 3** (Sedan)
   - Major refresh in 2024: Updated interior and exterior design
   - Entry-level sedan offering
   - Minimal changes expected for 2025

3. **Model X** (SUV)
   - Last major overhaul: 2021
   - Premium SUV with falcon-wing doors
   - No significant updates planned for 2025

4. **Model Y** (Crossover SUV)
   - New Standard Rear-Wheel Drive variant added (2025): $39,990 MSRP
   - EPA-estimated 321 miles of range
   - Best-selling Tesla model globally

#### Cybertruck (Pickup)
- **Launched:** Late 2023/Early 2024
- **2025 Updates:**
  - New rear-wheel-drive base model (lower price, reduced range)
  - Range extender available mid-2025: $16,000 add-on battery in bed
  - Range up to 445 miles with range extender
- Revolutionary stainless steel exoskeleton design

## Main Competitors & Differentiation

### BYD (Build Your Dreams) - China
**Differentiation Strategy:**
- Vertically integrated supply chain including battery production
- Massive cost advantages through local manufacturing
- Volume leader: Expected to produce 4M+ vehicles in 2025 vs Tesla's 2.5-3M target
- Profitability at lower price points
- Broad product portfolio across multiple segments

### Rivian - United States
**Differentiation Strategy:**
- Focus on adventure/lifestyle EVs
- Premium off-road performance
- Innovative features: Gear tunnel storage, camp kitchen
- Target market: Outdoor enthusiasts
- Upcoming R2 and R3 models will be more affordable mass-market offerings

### Lucid Motors - United States
**Differentiation Strategy:**
- Ultra-luxury performance positioning
- Industry-leading range: Lucid Air Grand Touring offers 512-mile range
- Premium interior and technology
- Expanding into more affordable segments
- Investment in autonomous driving technology

### NIO - China
**Differentiation Strategy:**
- Premium Chinese EV brand
- Battery-as-a-Service (BaaS) model: Subscribe to batteries vs. purchasing
- Extensive battery swap network: 2,500+ swap stations globally
- Reduces upfront costs and addresses range anxiety
- Focus on SUVs and sedans

### XPeng - China
**Differentiation Strategy:**
- Aggressive autonomous driving R&D investment
- Advanced self-driving software in P7, P5, G9, G6 models
- Price competitive: P7 sedan ~$35,000 (undercuts Model 3 in China)
- Global expansion: Plans to operate in 60+ countries by end of 2025
- Focus on immersive infotainment and driver-assistance systems

### Competitive Landscape Summary
Tesla faces increasing competition from multiple angles:
- **Cost leaders:** BYD and Chinese manufacturers with vertical integration
- **Luxury challengers:** Lucid with superior range and premium positioning
- **Niche players:** Rivian targeting adventure/lifestyle segment
- **Innovation competitors:** NIO (battery swapping), XPeng (autonomous driving)
- **Traditional automakers:** Ford, GM, VW, Mercedes, BMW entering EV market

## Key Annual Events

### Current Event Format (2024-2025)
Tesla has consolidated its major annual events into:

1. **Annual Shareholder Meeting**
   - Most recent: November 6, 2025, at 3:00 PM CT
   - Live streamed event
   - 2024 meeting featured: Cybercab unveiling, AI5 chip announcements, incorporation move to Texas

2. **Investor Day**
   - Last held: March 1, 2023, at Gigafactory Texas
   - Covered "Master Plan 3" and sustainable energy future
   - Live streamed globally

### Historical Events (Discontinued)
Tesla previously held specialized events that have been consolidated:
- **AI Day** - Focused on artificial intelligence and autonomous driving
- **Battery Day** - Focused on battery technology and manufacturing
- **Autonomy Day** - Focused on self-driving capabilities

These specialized topics are now covered within the regular shareholder meetings and investor presentations rather than as standalone events.
