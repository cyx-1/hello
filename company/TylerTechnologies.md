# Tyler Technologies, Inc.

## Company Overview

**Ticker Symbol:** NYSE: TYL
**Headquarters:** Plano, Texas
**Industry:** Government Software & Technology Services
**Employees:** ~7,400
**Website:** [tylertech.com](https://www.tylertech.com)

Tyler Technologies, Inc. is the largest software company in the United States focused solely on the public sector. The company provides integrated software and technology services to local, state, and federal government entities, as well as schools and other public sector organizations. Tyler's solutions are designed to help governments operate more efficiently and connect more transparently with citizens.

### Market Position
- Over 44,000 successful installations across 13,000+ locations
- Clients in all 50 states, Canada, the Caribbean, Australia, and other international locations
- Statewide enterprise contracts in 28 states
- 98% client retention rate
- S&P 500 company

## Products and Solutions

Tyler Technologies operates primarily in two business segments:

### Enterprise Software Solutions (ESS)
Provides software systems for government back-office functions including financial management, courts and justice processes, and citizen services.

### Appraisal and Tax Software Solutions and Services (ATSS)
Automates appraisal and assessment of real and personal property, plus property appraisal outsourcing services.

### Key Product Offerings

| Product | Category | Description |
|---------|----------|-------------|

## Key Milestones and Acquisitions

### Company History

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1966 | Founded as Saturn Industries by Joseph F. McKinney |
| 1968 | Acquired Tyler Pipe (manufacturer of iron pipes) |
| 1969 | Listed on New York Stock Exchange |
| 1970 | Changed name to Tyler Corporation |
| 1998 | Entered the government software market |
| 1999 | Changed name to Tyler Technologies |
| 2016 | Crossed $1 billion in annual revenue |
| 2018 | Acquired Socrata for ~$148 million |
| 2021 | Acquired NIC for $2.3 billion (largest acquisition) |
| 2024 | Exceeded $2 billion in annual revenue |

### Strategic Acquisitions

Tyler Technologies has completed acquisitions in 22 US states and 3 countries, primarily targeting software (35%) and information technology (32%) sectors.

**Major Acquisitions:**
- **NIC Inc. (2021)** - $2.3B - Digital government services and payment processing
- **Socrata (2018)** - $148M - Open data and data analytics platform
- **New World Systems (2015)** - Public safety and justice software
- **Brazos Technologies (2016)** - Courts and justice solutions

### Tyler's Competitive Advantages

**1. Public Sector Specialization**
- 100% focused on government market since 1998
- Deep domain expertise in government-specific requirements
- Purpose-built solutions vs. adapted commercial software

**2. Comprehensive Product Suite**
- End-to-end solutions across all government functions
- Integrated platform reducing complexity
- Single vendor for multiple departments

**3. Government-Specific Features**
- Fund accounting for pre-allocated budget tracking
- Built-in taxation tools (property tax, utility tax, revenue collection)
- Permitting and licensing workflows
- Compliance with regulatory requirements

**4. Scale and Market Leadership**
- Largest company focused solely on US public sector
- 44,000+ installations creating network effects
- 98% client retention rate
- S&P 500 stability

**5. Cloud Transition Leadership**
- Aggressive SaaS migration strategy
- Modern cloud architecture
- 97% of new deals are SaaS

**6. Strong Balance Sheet**
- Ability to make strategic acquisitions (e.g., NIC for $2.3B)
- Consistent revenue growth
- Expanding margins

### Differentiation Strategy

Tyler differentiates primarily through:

1. **Vertical Focus:** Unlike Oracle, Microsoft, or Workday, Tyler builds exclusively for government
2. **Integrated Ecosystem:** Solutions designed to work together across departments
3. **Long-term Relationships:** Deep client relationships with 98% retention
4. **Domain Expertise:** Staff with government experience understand public sector challenges
5. **Innovation Investment:** Continuous R&D in areas like AI, data analytics, and citizen experience
