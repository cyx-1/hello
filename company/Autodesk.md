## Main Products and Key Milestones

### AutoCAD
- **Launch:** December 1982
- **Type:** 2D/3D Computer-Aided Design (CAD)
- **Significance:** One of the first CAD programs for personal computers; democratized CAD access
- **First Year Revenue:** $1.4 million
- **Current Variants:** AutoCAD, AutoCAD LT, AutoCAD for Mac, AutoCAD Mobile
- **Key Milestones:**
  - 1982: Original release for DOS
  - 1992: Release 12 - industry standard
  - 2010: Cloud collaboration features
  - 2020: AutoCAD Web and Mobile apps enhanced

### Revit
- **Acquired:** February 2002 from Revit Technology Corporation for $133 million
- **Type:** Building Information Modeling (BIM) software
- **Significance:** Revolutionized architecture and construction with parametric building modeling
- **Key Milestones:**
  - 2002: Acquired by Autodesk
  - 2004: Revit Building released
  - 2006: Revit Structure, Revit MEP added
  - 2013: Revit unified into single platform
  - 2020: Enhanced cloud collaboration and generative design

### Fusion 360
- **Launch:** September 24, 2013 (beta in 2012)
- **Type:** Cloud-based 3D CAD/CAM/CAE/PCB platform
- **Significance:** First cloud-native integrated design and manufacturing platform
- **Initial Pricing:** $25/month
- **Key Milestones:**
  - 2012: Public beta released
  - 2013: Commercial launch
  - 2014: Inventor Fusion discontinued (consolidated into Fusion 360)
  - 2018: Generative design features added
  - 2020: Electronics design (PCB) integration
  - 2023: AI-powered design features

### Maya
- **Original Launch:** 1998 (by Alias)
- **Acquired:** 2006 via Alias Systems Corporation acquisition
- **Type:** 3D animation, modeling, simulation, rendering
- **Significance:** Industry standard for film VFX and game development
- **Notable Uses:** Avatar, Frozen, Game of Thrones, numerous AAA games
- **Key Milestones:**
  - 1998: Original launch by Alias
  - 2006: Autodesk acquires Alias
  - 2015: Bifrost procedural effects
  - 2020: OpenUSD integration
  - 2024: AI-assisted animation tools, Golaem acquisition

### 3ds Max
- **Original Launch:** 1996 as 3D Studio MAX (by Kinetix)
- **Acquired:** 1999 via Discreet Logic acquisition ($410 million deal)
- **Type:** 3D modeling, animation, rendering
- **Significance:** Dominant in architectural visualization and game development
- **Key Milestones:**
  - 1996: 3D Studio MAX released
  - 1999: Autodesk acquires via Discreet Logic
  - 2001: 3ds Max 4 with Character Studio
  - 2006: Rebranded "Autodesk 3ds Max"
  - 2020: GPU rendering improvements
  - 2024: OpenColorIO, OpenUSD updates

## Main Competitors and Differentiation

### Competitive Landscape Overview

The CAD/PLM market is dominated by an oligopoly of four major players: Autodesk, Dassault Systemes, Siemens, and PTC. Each has deep moats with extremely high switching costs.

### Dassault Systemes
**Headquarters:** Velizy-Villacoublay, France
**Key Products:** CATIA, SOLIDWORKS, 3DEXPERIENCE Platform

**Differentiation:**
- **Market Focus:** Enterprise-level aerospace, automotive, and defense
- **Strategy:** Premium PLM platform with comprehensive lifecycle management
- **Pricing:** High-end ($9,000-$65,000 per CATIA license)
- **Strengths:**
  - Deep aerospace/auto relationships (Boeing, Airbus, Tesla, BMW)
  - Full PLM suite including CAM, CAE, ergonomics
  - 3DEXPERIENCE cloud platform for enterprise collaboration
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Complex implementation
  - Slower growth (oldest of the four)
  - High cost limits SMB adoption

**SOLIDWORKS vs Autodesk:**
- Competes directly with Autodesk Inventor in mid-market
- More user-friendly than CATIA, affordable for SMBs
- Limited cloud/collaboration features compared to Fusion 360

### PTC (Parametric Technology Corporation)
**Key Products:** Creo, Windchill, ThingWorx, Onshape

**Differentiation:**
- **Market Focus:** Manufacturing, discrete industries
- **Strategy:** IoT and AR/VR integration with core CAD
- **Notable Acquisition:** Onshape (2019, $470M) - cloud-native CAD from SolidWorks founder
- **Strengths:**
  - Strong parametric modeling heritage
  - IoT platform (ThingWorx)
  - AR capabilities
  - Cloud-native with Onshape
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Smallest of the four major players
  - AR/VR investments haven't fully materialized
  - More limited industry breadth

### Bentley Systems
**Market Cap:** ~$12.54 billion
**Key Products:** MicroStation, ProjectWise, AssetWise, OpenRoad, OpenBridge, OpenRail

**Differentiation:**
- **Market Focus:** Infrastructure engineering (roads, bridges, rail, utilities)
- **Strategy:** Specialized infrastructure lifecycle management
- **Market Position:** Leader in infrastructure engineering tools
- **Notable Stats:** Used by 48/50 US states
- **Strengths:**
  - Deep infrastructure focus
  - Strong government/public sector relationships
  - Digital twin capabilities
  - Specialized tools for civil engineering
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Narrower product breadth
  - Less presence in manufacturing/M&E
  - Smaller overall market

### Autodesk's Competitive Advantages

1. **Broadest Product Portfolio:** Spans AEC, Manufacturing, and M&E
2. **SMB/Mid-Market Leadership:** AutoCAD accessible to individual users and small firms
3. **Cloud-First Strategy:** Fusion 360 pioneered cloud-native CAD
4. **BIM Dominance:** Revit is the industry standard for building design
5. **Strong Brand:** #238 in Global Top 1000 Brands
6. **Subscription Model:** Predictable revenue, 98% recurring
7. **Platform Convergence:** Design and Make Platform connecting workflows

### Emerging Competitors

- **Siemens PLM Software:** Strong in manufacturing with NX and Solid Edge
- **Trimble:** Construction technology and field solutions
- **Hexagon:** Measurement and manufacturing intelligence
- **Nemetschek Group:** European AEC software (Graphisoft, Vectorworks)
