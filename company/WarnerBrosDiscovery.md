# Warner Bros. Discovery, Inc.

## Company Type
**Public Company** - Trades on NASDAQ under ticker symbol **WBD**

## Overview
Warner Bros. Discovery, Inc. is a leading global media and entertainment company that creates and distributes content and brands across television, film, and streaming. The company was formed through the merger of WarnerMedia and Discovery, Inc., which was completed in April 2022.

## Main Competitors and Differentiation

### Netflix
- **Position**: Global streaming leader with 400+ million subscribers (adding 41M in 2024 alone)
- **Market Cap**: $400B+ (exceeds Disney, Comcast, Warner Bros Discovery, and Fox combined)
- **Strategy**: Internal content production, expanding into ads and sports
- **Differentiation**: Scale advantages, global reach, strong original content pipeline
- **Recent Development**: Announced $72B bid for WBD's streaming/studios division in December 2025

### Paramount Skydance
- **Subscribers**: 80 million (Paramount+)
- **Strategy**: Seeking merger with WBD to create scale competitor to Netflix
- **Vision**: Combined Paramount+ (80M) + Max (128M) = 208M subscribers to rival Netflix
- **Differentiation**: CEO David Ellison's vision for competing against Amazon, Disney, Netflix
- **Recent Development**: Launched hostile bid for Warner Bros Discovery in late 2025

### Comcast/NBCUniversal
- **Peacock Subscribers**: 41 million (as of Sept 2024)
- **Strategy**: Heavily invested in sports programming, lacking original content
- **Advantage**: Supplemental revenue from wireless internet and theme parks
- **Interest**: WBD intellectual property would boost Peacock's content library
- **Recent Development**: Submitted bid for WBD by November 2025

### Disney
- **Advantages**: Theme parks, ESPN sports network, established franchises
- **Differentiation**: Diversified revenue streams beyond streaming and linear TV

### Key Industry Challenges
- **Linear TV Decline**: Double-digit advertising drops in 2025
- **Over-reliance on Linear TV**: WBD and Paramount lack supplemental revenue (unlike Comcast's internet/parks or Disney's parks)
- **Debt Burden**: Industry consolidation driven by need to reduce debt and achieve scale
- **Streaming Profitability**: Recent positive trend - WBD streaming EBITDA reached $1.3B (from -$2.5B loss)

### Warner Bros Discovery's Differentiation
- **Premium Content**: HBO prestige programming, DC Comics IP, Harry Potter franchise
- **Streaming Turnaround**: Achieved profitability in streaming (Q3 2025: $2.47B adjusted EBITDA)
- **Debt Reduction**: Net leverage down to 3.3x EBITDA, paid down $1.2B in Q3 2025
- **Subscriber Growth**: Grew from 103M to 128M subscribers in one year (Q2 2024 to Q3 2025)
- **Content Library**: Extensive Warner Bros. film/TV catalog plus Discovery's factual/lifestyle programming
