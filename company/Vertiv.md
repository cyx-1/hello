# Vertiv Holdings Co.

## Company Overview

**Stock Ticker:** NYSE: VRT
**Company Type:** Public Company
**Industry:** Critical Digital Infrastructure & Data Center Solutions
**Headquarters:** Westerville, Ohio, United States

Vertiv Holdings Co designs, manufactures, and services critical digital infrastructure technologies and life cycle services for data centers, communication networks, and commercial and industrial environments in the Americas, the Asia Pacific, Europe, the Middle East, and Africa.

## Company History

### Early Origins (1946-1987)
- Founded in 1946 as Capitol Refrigeration Industries by Ralph Liebert (1918-1984) in Columbus, Ohio
- Ralph Liebert developed the first prototype precision air conditioner in his garage
- Rebranded as Liebert Corporation
- **1987:** Acquired by Emerson Electric in March, became a wholly owned subsidiary

### Emerson Network Power Era (2000-2016)
- **2000:** Emerson formed its Network Power (ENP) business unit, consolidating critical infrastructure technologies under a single brand
- Operated as Emerson Network Power for 16 years

### Platinum Equity Acquisition (2016)
- **December 2016:** Platinum Equity acquired ENP from Emerson Electric for over $4 billion
- Following the acquisition, the business was rebranded as **Vertiv**
- Emerson Electric retained a subordinated equity stake in the newly formed company

### Going Public via SPAC (2020)
- **February 10, 2020:** Vertiv became publicly traded on the NYSE through a merger with Goldman Sachs-led SPAC (GS Acquisition Holdings Corp)
- Transaction valued at approximately $5.3 billion
- SPAC sponsored by Goldman Sachs and David Cote (former CEO of Honeywell)
- **Ownership distribution at IPO:**
  - Platinum Equity: ~38%
  - Former GS Acquisition Holdings stockholders: ~25%
  - Private placement investors: ~37%

## Main Products and Services

### 1. Critical Power Solutions
**Uninterruptible Power Supplies (UPS):**
- Single-phase and three-phase UPS systems
- AI racks require UPS systems with higher power densities
- Handle loads from 10% idle to 150% overload

**Rack Power Distribution:**
- Vertiv™ PowerIT rack PDUs provide up to 57.6 kW of power distribution
- Monitored and switched models with advanced power management
- Load balancing and cybersecurity features

**PowerBar Track:**
- Overhead power distribution system for high-density AI and HPC environments
- Modular, open-track busbar architecture

**Integrated Rack Solutions:**
- Complete solutions including racks, power distribution, thermal systems
- Configurable integrated solutions
- Hardware and software for IT equipment management

### 2. Thermal Management Solutions
**Cooling Technologies:**
- Liquid cooling solutions (cold-plate and immersion cooling)
- Heat rejection systems
- Outdoor packaged systems
- Room cooling and in-row cooling
- Rack cooling systems
- Free cooling and evaporative free cooling
- Chillers
- Thermal control and monitoring

**AI-Focused Infrastructure:**
- Vertiv™ SmartIT OCP rack solution
- Configurable, high-capacity rack system
- Supports loads up to 142 kW
- Prefabricated configurations with validated power and cooling options
- OCP-compliant power, cooling, and rack ecosystem

**Low-GWP Offerings:**
- Expanding thermal management portfolio with low Global Warming Potential refrigerants in North America

### 3. Services and Support
- Preventative maintenance
- Acceptance testing
- Engineering and consulting
- Performance assessments
- Remote monitoring
- Training programs
- Spare parts management
- Critical digital infrastructure software

### 4. Strategic Partnerships
**Caterpillar Energy Collaboration (2025):**
- End-to-end power and cooling offerings for AI data centers
- Energy optimization solutions

### Main Competitors

| **Delta Electronics** | Power and thermal management solutions | Top 5 player in data center power market |

### Competitive Analysis

**Market Share:**
- Schneider Electric, ABB, Eaton, Vertiv, and Delta Electronics collectively account for 41-43% of total data center power market share
- In UPS market: Schneider Electric, Vertiv, Eaton, Huawei, and ABB collectively hold 40-42% market share
- Vertiv and Schneider Electric dominate the data center physical infrastructure market with nearly equal shares

**Vertiv's Competitive Advantages:**
- Comprehensive portfolio spanning power, cooling, and monitoring
- Specialized focus on digital infrastructure and edge computing
- Strong position in AI infrastructure with OCP-compliant solutions
- Rapidly gaining market share, closely trailing Schneider Electric's leading position

**Competitive Challenges:**
- Some competitors (like Eaton) have in-house manufacturing of critical components (e.g., circuit breakers), which can influence supply chain prioritization
- Schneider Electric's broader platform approach with EcoStruxure
