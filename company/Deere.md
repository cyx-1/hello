---

## Company Overview

Deere & Company, commonly known as John Deere, is one of the world's largest manufacturers of agricultural machinery, construction equipment, and forestry equipment. The company was founded in 1837 when blacksmith John Deere invented the first commercially successful steel plow that could cut through the sticky Midwestern prairie soil without clogging. Today, John Deere is the world's leading farm machinery manufacturer and the third-largest construction machinery manufacturer globally in terms of revenue.

## Main Products and Business Segments

John Deere operates through four primary business segments:

### 1. Production & Precision Agriculture (PPA)
**Revenue (FY 2023): ~$27B**

This is the company's largest segment, producing:
- Large tractors (8R and 9R series)
- Combine harvesters
- Cotton harvesters
- Sugarcane harvesters
- Sprayers and crop care equipment
- Precision agriculture technology solutions

### 2. Small Agriculture & Turf (SA&T)
**Revenue (FY 2023): ~$13B**

Products include:
- Compact and utility tractors
- Lawn mowers and residential equipment
- Golf course and sports turf equipment
- Snow removal equipment
- Gator utility vehicles

### 3. Construction & Forestry (C&F)
**Revenue (FY 2023): ~$15B (24% of total revenue)**

Products include:
- Crawler dozers and loaders
- Excavators
- Wheel loaders
- Backhoe loaders
- Skid-steer loaders
- Motor graders
- Forestry equipment (harvesters, forwarders, skidders, feller bunchers)

### 4. Financial Services
**Revenue (FY 2023): ~$6B**

Provides financing for equipment purchases, leasing, and insurance services.

## Main Competitors and Market Differentiation

### Competitive Landscape

John Deere holds approximately 15-18% global market share in agricultural equipment. Key competitors include:

| Company | Headquarters | 2023 Revenue | Key Strengths |
|---------|-------------|--------------|---------------|
| **Deere & Company** | USA | $61.5B | Market leader; Advanced autonomy and precision ag; Brand reputation for durability |
| **CNH Industrial** | Netherlands | $22.5B | Strong in Europe and South America; Diverse portfolio (Case IH, New Holland) |
| **Kubota Corporation** | Japan | $21.3B | Competitive pricing; Strong in compact tractors; Sustainability focus |
| **AGCO Corporation** | USA | $14.4B | Precision farming solutions (Fuse); Multiple brands (Massey Ferguson, Fendt, Challenger) |
| **CLAAS KGaA** | Germany | ~$5B | European market strength; Harvesting equipment expertise |

### Competitor Differentiation Strategies

**Kubota Corporation**
- More competitive pricing, especially in compact tractor segment
- Strong appeal to small/medium farms and hobby farmers
- Focus on sustainability with electric tractor development

**CNH Industrial**
- Robust market share in Europe and South America
- Diverse product portfolio across brands
- Strong focus on precision agriculture through acquisitions (e.g., Hemisphere GNSS in October 2023)

**AGCO Corporation**
- Growth through strategic acquisitions since founding in 1990
- Smart farming technologies (Fuse Connected Services)
- Tailored solutions for different regions and farming practices
- Brands include Massey Ferguson, Challenger, and Fendt

### John Deere's Competitive Advantages

1. **Technology Leadership**: Nearly 30 years of precision agriculture development; Leader in autonomous tractors and Operations Center digital platform
2. **Brand Reputation**: Longstanding reputation for durability and reliability dating back to 1837
3. **Vertical Integration**: Manufacturing, distribution, and financial services under one organization
4. **Global Dealer Network**: Extensive service and support infrastructure worldwide
5. **R&D Investment**: Continuous innovation in autonomy, electrification, and connected equipment
6. **Full-Line Offering**: Comprehensive equipment range from small residential to large commercial agriculture

## Summary

Deere & Company remains the global leader in agricultural machinery with a diversified presence in construction and forestry equipment. After record performance in FY 2023, the company has navigated a cyclical downturn in FY 2024 as market volumes returned to mid-cycle levels. Key strategic focus areas include autonomous equipment, precision agriculture technology, and sustainability initiatives. The company's strong brand reputation, extensive dealer network, and continuous innovation position it well for long-term growth despite near-term market challenges.
