# Invesco Ltd. (IVZ)

## Company Overview

**Company Type:** Public Company
**Stock Ticker:** IVZ (NYSE)
**Industry:** Asset Management
**Headquarters:** Atlanta, Georgia, USA
**Assets Under Management (AUM):** $2.17 trillion (as of October 31, 2025)

Invesco Ltd. is a publicly owned investment manager and one of the largest independent investment management firms globally. The company is best known for its Invesco QQQ ETF, one of the most widely traded exchange-traded funds in the world.

## Company History

### Origins and Founding

- **1935:** Charles Dexter McCoy founded Investment Counsel, Inc. in Atlanta, Georgia, with initial focus on investment advisory services and real estate investments
- **1959:** Company renamed to Citizens & Southern Investment Counseling, Inc., reflecting affiliation with Citizens & Southern National Bank
- **1978:** The "Invesco" name was officially adopted following the divestiture of Citizens & Southern National Bank's money management operations, led by Charles W. Brady
- **1988:** Acquired by British firm Britannia Arrow (London), which later took on the INVESCO name

### Key Milestones

- **1997:** Invesco PLC merged with AIM Investments, forming AMVESCAP PLC, creating one of the first truly global independent asset managers and significantly increasing Invesco's global reach
- **1999:** QQQ ETF launched on March 10, tracking the Nasdaq-100 Index
- **2004:** QQQ reached $10 billion in AUM and moved from American Stock Exchange to Nasdaq
- **2007:** Company reverted to the Invesco name; PowerShares (now part of Invesco) acquired QQQ from Nasdaq
- **2017:** QQQ reached $50 billion in AUM
- **2020:** QQQ crossed $100 billion AUM milestone; launched QQQM and QQQJ as part of the Innovation Suite
- **2024:** QQQ celebrated 25th anniversary with over $300 billion in AUM
- **2025:** Completed strategic partnership with Barings, launching Invesco Dynamic Credit Opportunity Fund (October 7, 2025)

## Main Products and Investment Offerings

### Flagship Products

#### **Invesco QQQ ETF (QQQ)**
- **Inception:** March 10, 1999
- **Description:** Tracks the Nasdaq-100 Index, providing access to 100 innovative companies
- **AUM:** Over $300 billion (as of 2024)
- **Expense Ratio:** 0.20%
- **Ranking:** 5th largest ETF globally, 2nd most-traded ETF in the US by average daily volume
- **Total AUM:** Approximately $409 billion

#### **Invesco QQQ Innovation Suite**
Launched to expand on QQQ's success, offering multiple ways to access the Nasdaq ecosystem:

- **QQQM (Invesco NASDAQ 100 ETF):** Lower-cost alternative to QQQ for long-term growth investors
- **QQA (Invesco QQQ Income Advantage ETF):** Provides consistent monthly income while maintaining Nasdaq-100 exposure
- **ESG Options:** Tracks Nasdaq-100 ESG Index and Nasdaq Next Generation 100 ESG Index

### Investment Platform Capabilities

Invesco offers a diversified platform spanning:
- **Beta Strategies:** Index-based ETFs and passive investments
- **Factor Strategies:** Smart beta and multi-factor approaches
- **Active Strategies:** Actively managed funds across asset classes
- **Asset Classes:**
  - ETFs & Index products
  - Fixed Income (Fundamental and Active)
  - Private Markets
  - Alternative investments

### Distribution Channels
- Financial advisors

- Retail investors through global distribution network

### Market Positioning and Differentiation

#### Invesco's Competitive Position

**Assets Under Management:** $2.17 trillion (as of October 2025)
- Ranks in the second-largest tier of US-based asset managers
- Significantly smaller than the "Big Three" but maintains substantial scale

**Differentiation Strategy:**

1. **Innovation Focus**
   - Strong association with innovation through QQQ's Nasdaq-100 exposure
   - Innovation Suite offering multiple access points to technology and growth companies

2. **Diversified Platform**
   - Comprehensive offering spanning beta, factor, and active strategies
   - Multi-asset class capabilities (equity, fixed income, alternatives, private markets)

3. **Global Distribution**
   - Presence across Americas (70%), EMEA (15%), and Asia Pacific (15%)
   - Strong growth in international markets, particularly China and India

4. **ETF Leadership**
   - QQQ is the 2nd most-traded ETF in the US
   - 5th largest ETF globally
   - Consistent ETF net inflows ($32.7B in 2023, major driver in 2024-2025)

5. **Client Segmentation**
   - Serves advisors, institutions, and retail investors
   - Multi-channel distribution strategy

#### Industry Context

**Market Dominance Statistics:**
- The "Big Three" (BlackRock, Vanguard, State Street) together manage nearly $11 trillion
- A 2019 Harvard Business Review study found these three are the largest listed owner of 88% of S&P 500 companies
- These firms control 74% of the equity ETF market

**Invesco's Challenge:**
- Competes against significantly larger competitors with greater resources
- Must differentiate through innovation, product specialization, and service quality
- Leverages QQQ brand strength and innovation positioning
