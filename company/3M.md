# 3M Company (MMM)

## Company Overview

3M Company (originally Minnesota Mining and Manufacturing Company) is an American multinational conglomerate corporation operating in the fields of industry, worker safety, and consumer goods. Founded in 1902 in Two Harbors, Minnesota, 3M produces over 60,000 products across multiple sectors including adhesives, abrasives, laminates, personal protective equipment, electronic materials, and consumer goods.

**Stock Symbol:** NYSE: MMM
**Headquarters:** St. Paul, Minnesota, USA
**CEO:** William "Bill" Brown (appointed May 2024)
**Employees:** ~85,000 (post-Solventum spinoff)

## Business Segments

Following the Solventum spinoff in April 2024, 3M now operates in three main business segments:

### Safety and Industrial
**2024 Revenue:** $10.96B (45.16% of total)
**2024 Organic Growth:** 0.7%

Products include:
- Personal protective equipment (N95 respirators, hearing/eye protection)
- Industrial adhesives and tapes
- Abrasives
- Roofing granules
- Workplace safety solutions
- Reflective materials for apparel and footwear

### Transportation and Electronics
**2024 Revenue:** $8.38B (34.53% of total)
**2024 Organic Growth:** -1.0%

Products include:
- Automotive components
- Aerospace materials
- Electronics connecting and insulating materials
- Optical films
- Display enhancement films
- Advanced materials for semiconductors

### Consumer
**2024 Revenue:** $4.93B (20.32% of total)
**2024 Organic Growth:** -1.2%

Products include:
- Post-it Notes
- Scotch Tape
- Scotch-Brite cleaning products
- Command adhesive products
- Filtrete air filters
- Nexcare wound care
- O-Cel-O sponges

## Main Products and Key Milestones

### Iconic Product Portfolio

| Product | Category | Key Milestone |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| **Scotch Tape** | Consumer | Invented in 1930 by Richard Drew; first transparent adhesive tape |
| **Post-it Notes** | Consumer | Launched nationally in 1980; invented by Spencer Silver (adhesive, 1968) and Art Fry (concept, 1974) |
| **N95 Respirator** | Safety | Developed by 3M, approved in 1972; critical during COVID-19 pandemic |
| **Scotchgard** | Consumer | Fabric protector with water and stain resistance |
| **Command Strips** | Consumer | Damage-free hanging solutions |
| **Filtrete** | Consumer | Home air filtration systems |

### Major Corporate Milestones

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1902 | Founded as Minnesota Mining and Manufacturing Company |
| 1925 | Richard Drew invents masking tape |
| 1929 | Company name shortened to "3M" |
| 1930 | Scotch Transparent Tape invented |
| 1972 | N95 respirator approved |
| 1980 | Post-it Notes national launch |
| 2022 | Announced Healthcare spinoff |

### Innovation Culture

3M is renowned for its "15% Time" policy, encouraging employees to spend 15% of their work time on personal innovation projects. This policy has led to iconic products including Post-it Notes, Scotch Tape, and wireless electronic stethoscopes.

## Competitors and Market Differentiation

### Main Competitors

| Competitor | Overlap Areas | Key Differentiation |
|------------|---------------|---------------------|

| **Danaher** | Life sciences, diagnostics | Strategic acquisitions in science and technology innovation |
| **Avery Dennison** | Adhesive materials, labels | Specialization in labeling and functional materials |

### 3M's Competitive Advantages

1. **Product Diversification**
   - Over 60,000 products across multiple industries
   - Reduces dependence on any single market

2. **R&D Investment**
   - Historically invests 5-6% of sales in R&D
   - Strong innovation pipeline

3. **Intellectual Property**
   - Extensive patent portfolio
   - Platform technologies that span multiple industries

4. **Brand Recognition**
   - Iconic consumer brands (Post-it, Scotch, Command)
   - Trusted industrial and safety products

5. **Global Manufacturing**
   - Worldwide production capabilities
   - Supply chain resilience

### Competitive Challenges

- **PFAS Litigation**: Ongoing legal challenges related to "forever chemicals"
- **Healthcare Spinoff Impact**: Loss of diversification from Solventum separation
- **Market Competition**: Increasing competition in safety equipment and industrial materials
- **Margin Pressure**: Need to balance innovation investment with operational efficiency

## Recent Strategic Developments

### Solventum Spinoff (April 2024)

- Completed spin-off of Healthcare business as Solventum Corporation (NYSE: SOLV)
- Solventum had 2023 sales of $8.2 billion with 22,000 employees
- 3M retained 19.9% stake to be monetized within five years
- Dividend reduced 53.6% (from $1.51 to $0.70 per share quarterly) post-spinoff

### Leadership Transition

- William "Bill" Brown appointed CEO in May 2024
- Focus on "back to basics" approach
- Emphasis on operational excellence and innovation

### 2025 Strategic Priorities

- Streamlined industrial science focus
- Improved operational performance
- Enhanced margin expansion
- Continued innovation investment
