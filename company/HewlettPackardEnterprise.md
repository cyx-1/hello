# Hewlett Packard Enterprise (HPE)

## Company Overview

**Company Name:** Hewlett Packard Enterprise Company
**Ticker Symbol:** HPE (NYSE)
**Headquarters:** Spring, Texas, USA
**Founded:** November 1, 2015
**CEO:** Antonio Neri (since February 1, 2018)
**Company Type:** Public Company
**Industry:** Information Technology, Enterprise Computing

Hewlett Packard Enterprise Company (HPE) is an American multinational information technology company that provides servers, storage, networking, containerization software, and consulting and support services. HPE was created as part of the splitting of the Hewlett-Packard company in 2015, with HP Inc. retaining the personal computing and printing businesses while HPE focused on enterprise products and services.

## Main Products and Services

### HPE GreenLake
HPE's flagship as-a-service platform delivering a cloud experience for on-premises and hybrid cloud environments.

**Key Features:**
- IT-as-a-Service (ITaaS) managed cloud experience
- 50+ cloud services including ML Ops, analytics, and SAP HANA
- Consumption-based pricing model
- Unified management across hybrid environments

**Milestones:**
- June 2018: Initial launch as GreenLake Hybrid Cloud
- 2019: CEO Neri announces goal to offer all products as-a-service by 2022
- 2025: GreenLake Intelligence launched with AI-powered agents for networking, observability, and workload optimization

### HPE ProLiant Servers
Industry-leading server portfolio for enterprise computing.

**Product Lines:**
- ProLiant Rack and Tower servers
- ProLiant Gen12 (launching 2025) with AI automation and federal security certification
- Up to 65% power cost savings with latest generation

Comprehensive networking solutions acquired in 2015 for approximately $3 billion.

**Products:**

- Network security and analytics

### High Performance Computing (HPC)
**Products:**
- HPE Cray EX supercomputers
- HPE Cray XD systems
- Solutions for AI/ML workloads

### Storage Solutions
**Products:**

- HPE Primera

### Other Products

## Recent Major Acquisition: Juniper Networks

**Deal Value:** $14 billion (all-cash, $40 per share)
**Announced:** January 9, 2024
**Completed:** July 2, 2025

### Strategic Rationale
- Doubles the size of HPE's networking business
- Creates comprehensive AI-driven networking portfolio
- Positions HPE to compete with Cisco and Nvidia in data center/AI markets
- Adds Juniper's Mist AI capabilities

### Regulatory Settlement
- DOJ initially sued to block the deal citing competition concerns
- Settlement required:
  - Divestiture of HPE's Aruba Instant On business (within 180 days)
  - Non-exclusive licensing of Juniper's Mist AIOps source code

### Leadership
- Rami Rahim (former Juniper CEO) now leads combined HPE Networking business

## Competitive Landscape

### Main Competitors

| Competitor | Overlap Areas | Key Differentiators |
|------------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Dell Technologies** | Servers, Storage, Networking | Comprehensive client-to-enterprise portfolio; strong enterprise relationships; AI infrastructure focus |
| **Cisco Systems** | Networking, Data Center | Dominant in networking/communications; UCS servers; emphasis on security and cloud software |
| **IBM** | Enterprise IT, HPC, Hybrid Cloud | Mainframes, enterprise AI (Watson); consulting services; Red Hat OpenShift |
| **Lenovo** | Servers, Data Center | Cost-effectiveness; acquired IBM x86 server business; strong in emerging markets |
| **Huawei** | Servers, Networking, Storage | Strong in telecommunications; competitive pricing; significant presence in Asia/EMEA |

### HPE's Competitive Differentiation

**1. GreenLake As-a-Service Model**
- Consumption-based pricing for on-premises infrastructure
- Cloud-like experience with data sovereignty benefits
- Alternative to public cloud for hybrid environments

**2. Full-Stack Hybrid Cloud Strategy**
- Integrated compute, storage, and networking portfolio
- End-to-end solutions from edge to cloud
- Strong partnerships (AMD EPYC, SAP, etc.)

**3. AI and HPC Leadership**
- HPE Cray supercomputers powering world's largest systems
- AI-native networking through Juniper acquisition
- ProLiant Gen12 with built-in AI automation

**4. Services Expertise (Pointnext)**
- Professional services and support
- Advisory and transformation services
- Managed services capabilities

**5. Networking Portfolio Expansion**
- Combined Aruba + Juniper creates comprehensive networking stack
- AI-driven network management
- Competitive alternative to Cisco

### Market Position
- Top position in worldwide server market (tied with Dell)
- Leading edge-to-cloud platform provider
- Strong presence in high-performance computing
- Growing AI infrastructure business
