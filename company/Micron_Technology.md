## Main Products and Key Milestones

### Core Product Categories

1. **DRAM (Dynamic Random Access Memory)**
   - DDR4, DDR5, LPDDR5/5X
   - High Bandwidth Memory (HBM3E)
   - Graphics DRAM (GDDR6/7)

2. **NAND Flash Memory**
   - Data center SSDs (Micron 9400 NVMe)
   - Consumer SSDs (Crucial brand)
   - Mobile storage

3. **High Bandwidth Memory (HBM)**
   - HBM3E 8H (24GB) - production began Feb 2024
   - HBM3E 12H (36GB) - volume ramp early 2025

### Key Milestones (2022-2024)

| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| Sep 2022 | Record $30.8B annual revenue |
| Feb 2024 | HBM3E mass production began for NVIDIA H200 |
| Q3 2024 | Over $100M in HBM sales achieved |
| Q4 2024 | Data center SSD revenue exceeded $1B quarterly (first time) |
| Q4 2024 | Record NAND revenue and Storage BU revenue |
| Dec 2024 | $6.165B CHIPS Act funding secured |
| 2024 | 60,000 lifetime patents milestone |

### HBM Strategy and Roadmap

- **HBM3E 8H**: Shipping to NVIDIA for H200 and B200/GB200 platforms
- **HBM3E 12H**: For AMD MI325X/MI355X and NVIDIA B300-series
- **HBM4**: Mass production planned for 2026
- **Target**: 20%+ HBM market share, matching DRAM share
- **Advantage**: 30% less power consumption than competitors

### CHIPS Act Investments

- **Total CHIPS Act Funding**: $6.165B (plus $275M for Virginia)
- **Idaho Investment**: $25B (first fab output in 2027)
- **New York Investment**: $100B
- **Jobs Created**: 20,000 projected

## Main Competitors and Differentiation

### Samsung Electronics (South Korea)
- **Market Position**: #1 in DRAM and NAND
- **Strengths**: Vertically integrated, massive scale, diverse portfolio

### SK Hynix (South Korea)
- **Market Position**: #2 in DRAM, #1 in HBM
- **Strengths**: 238-layer 4D NAND technology, HBM leadership

### Micron's Differentiation Strategy

1. **Technology Leadership**
   - Skipped HBM3, went directly to HBM3E
   - 12-stack HBM3E: 20% less power, 50% more capacity vs. competition

2. **U.S. Manufacturing**
   - Only U.S.-based memory manufacturer
   - CHIPS Act beneficiary for domestic production

3. **Power Efficiency**
   - HBM3E consumes 30% less power than competitors

4. **Customer Diversification**
   - Strong relationships with NVIDIA, AMD
   - Growing automotive and embedded presence

5. **Financial Discipline**
   - $9.16B cash position (end of FY2024)

## Outlook and Growth Drivers

1. **AI/Data Center**: Primary growth catalyst; HBM revenue expected to reach "multiple billions" in FY2025
2. **HBM Market**: Expected to double from $17B (2024) to $34B (2025)
3. **U.S. Manufacturing**: $125B total investment planned through CHIPS Act support
4. **Product Mix**: Shift toward higher-margin HBM and data center products
5. **Automotive**: Growing embedded business with autonomous driving demand
