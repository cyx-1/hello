# Advanced Micro Devices, Inc. (AMD)

## Company Overview

**Type:** Public Company (NASDAQ: AMD)
**Headquarters:** Santa Clara, California, USA (headquarters); Austin, Texas (CEO office)
**Founded:** May 1, 1969 by Jerry Sanders and colleagues from Fairchild Semiconductor
**CEO:** Dr. Lisa Su (since October 2014)
**Industry:** Semiconductors, CPUs, GPUs, AI Accelerators

Advanced Micro Devices is an American multinational semiconductor company that develops computer processors and related technologies for business and consumer markets. AMD competes primarily with Intel in CPUs and NVIDIA in GPUs.

## Main Products and Key Milestones

### Data Center Products
- **AMD EPYC:** Server CPUs (5th Gen "Turin" latest)
- **AMD Instinct MI300:** AI accelerators with industry-leading memory
- **Pensando DPUs:** Data processing units for cloud and enterprise
- **Pensando Pollara NICs:** High-performance network adapters

### Client Products
- **AMD Ryzen:** Desktop and mobile processors
- **AMD Ryzen AI:** Processors with dedicated NPU for AI workloads
- **AMD Ryzen PRO:** Enterprise-focused processors

### Gaming Products
- **AMD Radeon:** Consumer graphics cards
- **Semi-Custom:** Chips for PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S

### Embedded Products
- **Xilinx FPGAs:** Field-programmable gate arrays
- **Versal:** Adaptive compute acceleration platforms

### Key Milestones
- **1969:** Founded in Silicon Valley
- **2014:** Lisa Su becomes CEO
- **2017:** Launched Ryzen, beginning turnaround
- **2019:** Launched 7nm EPYC, gaining server market share
- **2022:** Completed $49B acquisition of Xilinx
- **December 2023:** Launched Instinct MI300X AI accelerator
- **2024:** AI accelerator revenue reached $5B (initially guided $3.5B)
- **2024:** Lisa Su named Time's CEO of the Year
- **2024:** Announced MI325X, MI350, MI400 roadmap

## AI Accelerator Performance

### Instinct MI300X Specifications
- **Memory:** 192GB HBM3 (industry-leading)
- **Memory Bandwidth:** 5.3 TB/s (vs 3.3 TB/s on NVIDIA H100)
- **Advantage:** Can run larger AI models without model sharding

### Instinct MI325X (Q4 2024)
- **Memory:** 288GB HBM3E
- **Memory Bandwidth:** 6 TB/s
- **Positioning:** 2x memory capacity vs competition

### Customer Adoption
- Microsoft purchased ~97,000 AMD GPUs in 2024 (1 in 6 of total GPU purchases)
- Meta: 43% of GPU purchases were AMD (173,000 units)

## Competitors and Differentiation

### CPU Competition (vs Intel)

| Aspect | AMD | Intel |
|--------|-----|-------|
| **Performance** | Leading in most benchmarks | Catching up with new architectures |
| **Market Share** | 27% and growing | 61% and declining |
| **Manufacturing** | TSMC partnership | Own foundries (struggling) |
| **Power Efficiency** | Generally better | Improving with new nodes |

### GPU/AI Competition (vs NVIDIA)

| Aspect | AMD | NVIDIA |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Market Share** | ~5% in AI | ~95% in AI |
| **Memory** | Advantage (192-288GB) | 80-141GB (H100/H200) |
| **Software Ecosystem** | ROCm (improving) | CUDA (dominant) |
| **Pricing** | Generally lower | Premium pricing |

### Competitive Advantages
1. **Chiplet Architecture:** Cost-effective, scalable designs
2. **Memory Leadership:** MI300X offers more HBM than competitors
3. **TSMC Partnership:** Access to leading-edge manufacturing
4. **Lisa Su Leadership:** Exceptional execution and strategic vision
5. **Open Source:** ROCm provides alternative to CUDA lock-in

### Competitive Challenges
- NVIDIA's CUDA software ecosystem is deeply entrenched
- Intel has more resources for R&D and marketing
- Gaming and embedded segments facing cyclical weakness

## Software Ecosystem

### ROCm (Radeon Open Compute)
- Open-source software platform for GPU computing
- Over 1 million AI models now run on AMD Instinct (3x since MI300X launch)
- Doubled MI300X inference and training performance through software optimization
- Key focus area: thousands of engineers building out software division
