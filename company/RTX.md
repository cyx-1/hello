# RTX Corporation

## Company Overview

RTX Corporation is an American aerospace and defense conglomerate headquartered in Arlington, Virginia. The company was formed in April 2020 through the merger of Raytheon Company and United Technologies Corporation and was renamed from Raytheon Technologies to RTX Corporation in July 2023.

RTX is one of the largest aerospace and defense manufacturers in the world by revenue and market capitalization, with more than 185,000 global employees. The company operates through three primary business segments: Collins Aerospace, Pratt & Whitney, and Raytheon.

**Stock Symbol:** NYSE: RTX
**Headquarters:** Arlington, Virginia, USA
**Industry:** Aerospace & Defense

## Business Segments & Main Products

### 1. Collins Aerospace
**2024 Revenue: $28.28B**

Collins Aerospace specializes in advanced structures, avionics, interiors, mission systems, and power and control systems serving commercial, regional, business aviation, and military sectors.

**Key Products:**
- Avionics and flight systems
- Aircraft cabin interiors and seating
- Mission systems for military applications
- Mechanical systems (landing gear, actuation systems)
- Information management services
- Power and control systems

**Key Milestones:**
- Leading supplier for both commercial and military aerospace platforms
- Major supplier for Boeing 787, Airbus A350, and F-35 programs
- Integrated systems for next-generation aircraft

### 2. Pratt & Whitney
**2024 Revenue: $28.07B**

Pratt & Whitney designs, manufactures, and services aircraft engines and auxiliary power systems for commercial, military, and business aircraft.

**Key Products:**
- **Commercial Engines:**
  - Geared Turbofan (GTF) engine family (PW1000G series)
  - PW4000 engines for wide-body aircraft
  - V2500 engines (joint venture with MTU)

- **Military Engines:**
  - F135 engine (sole power plant for F-35 Lightning II)
  - F119 engine (powers F-22 Raptor)
  - F117 engine (powers C-17 Globemaster III)
  - F100 engine (powers F-15 and F-16)
  - Engine for B-21 Raider long-range strike bomber

**Key Milestones:**
- **March 2023:** Awarded $5.2B contract for F135 engine Lots 15-17 production (278-518 engines)
- **September 2024:** Awarded up to $1.3B for F135 Engine Core Upgrade (ECU) program
- Over 1,200 F135 production engines delivered with 900,000+ flight hours
- GTF engines power over 1,400 aircraft in service globally

### 3. Raytheon
**2024 Revenue: $26.71B**

Raytheon develops and produces missiles, defense systems, sensors, and advanced technologies for defense and government customers.

**Key Products:**
- **Air & Missile Defense:**
  - Patriot missile defense system
  - NASAMS air defense system
  - THAAD components
  - Lower Tier Air and Missile Defense Sensor (LTAMDS)
  - GhostEye radar systems

- **Precision Weapons:**
  - AMRAAM (Advanced Medium-Range Air-to-Air Missile)
  - AIM-9X Sidewinder
  - Tomahawk cruise missile
  - Paveway laser-guided bombs
  - AGM-65 Maverick
  - TOW anti-tank missiles
  - GBU-28 bunker buster bombs

- **Sensors & Cyber:**
  - Advanced radar systems
  - Electro-optical/infrared systems
  - Cybersecurity solutions

**Key Milestones:**
- Secured $1.2B contract to supply Germany with additional Patriot systems (2024)
- NASAMS selected by multiple NATO countries
- GhostEye radar development partnership with Norway announced (2025)

## Main Competitors and Differentiation

### 1. Lockheed Martin
**2024 Revenue:** ~$71B
**Market Cap:** ~$140B

**Competitive Position:**
- World's largest defense contractor
- Primary F-35 airframe manufacturer (RTX provides F135 engines)
- Strong in hypersonics, cybersecurity, and directed energy weapons

**RTX Differentiation:** RTX has stronger commercial aerospace presence through Pratt & Whitney and Collins Aerospace, while Lockheed Martin is primarily defense-focused.

### 2. Boeing
**2024 Revenue:** ~$78B
**Market Cap:** ~$140B

**Competitive Position:**
- Largest U.S. commercial aircraft manufacturer
- Defense, Space & Security division includes F/A-18, KC-46, Apache
- Space Launch System (SLS) for NASA

**RTX Differentiation:** RTX provides critical components to Boeing aircraft (Collins avionics, Pratt engines) rather than competing directly in airframe manufacturing. RTX has stronger missile and defense systems portfolio.

### 3. Northrop Grumman
**2024 Revenue:** ~$41B
**Market Cap:** ~$80B

**Competitive Position:**
- B-21 Raider stealth bomber prime contractor
- Global Hawk UAV and space systems
- Strong in autonomous systems and space

**RTX Differentiation:** RTX has broader commercial aerospace presence and larger missiles portfolio; Northrop is more focused on advanced platforms and space.

### 4. General Dynamics
**2024 Revenue:** ~$44B
**Market Cap:** ~$85B

**Competitive Position:**
- Gulfstream business jets
- Combat vehicles (Abrams tank)
- IT solutions and submarines

**RTX Differentiation:** RTX has stronger position in commercial aviation propulsion and air defense systems; General Dynamics leads in land systems and submarines.

### 5. Airbus
**2024 Revenue:** ~$84B
**Market Cap:** ~$130B

**Competitive Position:**
- World's largest commercial aircraft manufacturer by deliveries
- Airbus Defence and Space division
- European government backing

**RTX Differentiation:** RTX supplies engines and systems to Airbus aircraft; Airbus competes more with Boeing in airframes while RTX is primarily a systems and propulsion supplier.

### Competitive Advantages

1. **Diversified Portfolio:** Balanced across commercial aerospace and defense
2. **Engine Technology Leadership:** GTF engines offer 16%+ fuel efficiency improvement
3. **Missile & Defense Dominance:** Patriot system is the world's most deployed air defense
4. **Strong Backlog:** $218B provides multi-year revenue visibility
5. **Supply Chain Integration:** Vertical integration across propulsion, avionics, and systems
6. **R&D Investment:** $7.5B+ planned for 2025
