# GE Vernova Inc. (NYSE: GEV)

## Company Overview

GE Vernova Inc. is a global energy company that was spun off from General Electric on April 2, 2024, and began trading on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol "GEV." The company was formed from the merger and subsequent spin-off of General Electric's energy businesses: GE Power, GE Renewable Energy, GE Digital, and GE Energy Financial Services.

**Headquarters:** Cambridge, Massachusetts, USA
**Employees:** 80,000+ across 140+ countries
**CEO:** Scott Strazik
**Market Cap:** ~$150 billion (as of late 2025)

GE Vernova's installed base of technologies generates approximately 25-30% of the world's electricity, with over 7,000 gas turbines and approximately 57,000 wind turbines globally.

## Business Segments and Main Products

### 1. Power Segment
**Market Position:** Largest installed fleet of gas turbines worldwide (~50% global market share in heavy-duty gas turbines)

**Key Products:**
- **HA-class Gas Turbines** - Industry-leading efficiency rates above 64%, including the 9HA (world's most efficient combined-cycle power plant core)
- **9E.03 Heavy-duty Gas Turbine** - Versatile for various duty cycles from desert to arctic conditions
- **7E.03 Gas Turbine** - Industry leader for 60 Hz industrial power applications
- **LM6000VELOX Aeroderivative** - 5-minute fast start, 100% hydrogen capability for grid stabilization
- **Steam Power Systems** - Serving industrial and power plant operations
- **Hydroelectric Systems** - Turbines, generators, and control systems

### 2. Wind Segment
**Market Position:** 120+ GW installed capacity, largest installed base of onshore wind turbines in the United States

**Key Products:**
- **Haliade-X Offshore Wind Turbine** - 250-meter rotor, one of the world's most powerful offshore turbines
- **Cypress Onshore Turbine** - 4MW-5MW capacity, unique two-piece blade design for better logistics
- **3.6-MW 154m Turbine** - For megawatt-constrained applications
- **6.1-MW 158m Turbine** - For land-constrained applications
- **LM Wind Power** - Leading wind turbine blade manufacturer

### 3. Electrification Segment
**Key Products and Services:**
- **GridOS** - Grid orchestration portfolio for planning, operating, and transacting
- **High-Voltage Switchgear** - Strong demand in US and Europe
- **Power Transformers** - Critical grid infrastructure
- **Solar & Storage Solutions** - Battery storage and hybrid systems
- **Power Conversion Systems** - For various industrial applications
- **BWRX-300** - Small modular reactor (300 MW carbon-free electricity)

## Competitive Landscape

### Main Competitors

| Competitor | Headquarters | Key Strengths | Market Focus |
|------------|--------------|---------------|--------------|
| **Siemens Energy** | Germany | Gas turbines, grid tech, offshore wind (via Gamesa) | Full energy portfolio |
| **Vestas** | Denmark | Largest wind turbine manufacturer, 193+ GW installed | Pure-play wind |
| **Mitsubishi Power** | Japan | Heavy industrial expertise, hydrogen technology | Gas turbines, hydrogen |
| **Goldwind** | China | Cost leadership, domestic Chinese market | Wind turbines |
| **Nordex** | Germany | Mid-size onshore wind turbines | Onshore wind |

### Competitive Positioning

**GE Vernova Strengths:**
- Largest installed base of gas turbines globally (50%+ of fleet)
- 96% US onshore wind market share (with Vestas)
- Comprehensive portfolio spanning gas, wind, hydro, grid, and nuclear
- $120+ billion backlog stretching into 2028
- Strong services revenue from large installed base
- Leading-edge technology (HA turbines, Haliade-X, BWRX-300)

**Key Differentiators:**
- **vs. Siemens Energy**: Stronger US presence, larger gas turbine installed base
- **vs. Vestas**: Broader portfolio beyond wind, services ecosystem
- **vs. Mitsubishi Power**: Larger global footprint, wind capabilities
- **vs. Chinese competitors**: Technology leadership, western market access

### Market Share by Segment

**Gas Power:**
- GE Vernova, Siemens Energy, and Mitsubishi collectively hold 60%+ of gas turbine MRO revenue
- GE Vernova leads in heavy-duty gas turbines with ~40% global market share
- ~55 GW of gas turbine orders in backlog

**Wind Power:**
- **Onshore (US)**: GE Vernova + Vestas = 96% market share
- **Offshore**: Siemens Gamesa #1 (4.3 GW), GE Vernova #2 (1.9 GW), Vestas #3 (1.5 GW)
- **Global Onshore**: Vestas leads, followed by Siemens Gamesa, GE Vernova, Goldwind

**Electrification:**
- Growing segment with 21% organic revenue growth in recent quarters
- Strong demand for transformers and switchgear in US and Europe
- Strategic acquisition of Prolec GE to expand transformer capacity

## Strategic Outlook

### Long-Term Financial Targets (2028)
- Adjusted EBITDA margin: 14%
- Free cash flow conversion: ~100%
- EPS growth: 34% in 2025, 71% by 2026 (to $12.77)

### Growth Drivers
1. **Energy Transition**: Decarbonization driving wind and grid investments
2. **Data Center Demand**: AI/cloud computing requiring massive power infrastructure
3. **Grid Modernization**: Aging infrastructure replacement and upgrades
4. **Gas-to-Renewables Bridge**: Gas turbines supporting intermittent renewables
5. **Services Expansion**: Growing installed base generating recurring revenue

### Key Risks
- Offshore wind project delays and cost overruns
- Supply chain constraints for transformers and switchgear
- Policy uncertainty around renewable energy incentives
- Competition from Chinese manufacturers
- Execution risk on Wind segment turnaround

## Summary

GE Vernova has successfully transitioned from a loss-making division of GE to a profitable, standalone energy company. The company is well-positioned to benefit from global electrification trends and the energy transition, with a comprehensive portfolio spanning gas power, wind energy, and grid solutions. Its massive installed base provides a steady stream of services revenue while new equipment orders are driving growth. The company's stock has appreciated over 300% since its April 2024 IPO, reflecting investor confidence in its turnaround and growth potential.
