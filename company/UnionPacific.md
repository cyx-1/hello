# Union Pacific Corporation (UNP)

## Company Overview

Union Pacific Corporation is one of America's leading transportation companies, operating the largest railroad network in the United States. The company's freight railroad operates over 32,200 miles of track across 23 states in the western two-thirds of the United States. Headquartered in Omaha, Nebraska, Union Pacific connects major West Coast and Gulf Coast ports to eastern gateways, serves all six major Mexico gateways, and links with Canada's rail systems.

**Stock Symbol:** UNP (NYSE)
**Headquarters:** Omaha, Nebraska
**Founded:** July 1, 1862
**Employees:** ~30,000+

## Main Products and Services

### Business Segments

Union Pacific organizes its freight operations into three primary business segments:

#### 1. Bulk Segment
- **Products:** Grain, fertilizer, food, coal, renewables
- **2024 Performance:** Revenue carloads up 5%, led by fertilizer (+3%), grain and chemicals (+8%)
- **Key Markets:** Agricultural exporters, food processors, energy companies

#### 2. Industrial Segment
- **Products:** Chemicals, plastics, metals, minerals, forest products, petroleum, construction materials
- **2024 Performance:** Strong growth in chemicals sector
- **Key Markets:** Chemical manufacturers, steel producers, construction industry

#### 3. Premium Segment
- **Products:** Automotive (finished vehicles and parts), intermodal containers
- **2024 Performance:** Accounts for ~31% of total freight revenues; intermodal traffic increased 16% in Q4 2024
- **Key Markets:** Auto manufacturers, retailers, international shippers

### Key Services

- **Freight Transportation:** Movement of commodities across 32,200 miles of track
- **Intermodal Services:** Truck trailer and container transportation combining rail and truck
- **Cross-Border Operations:** Only railroad serving all six U.S./Mexico gateways
- **Logistics Solutions:** End-to-end supply chain support

## Main Competitors and Differentiation

### Primary Competitors

| Company | Market Cap | Track Miles | Geographic Focus | Key Differentiator |
|---------|-----------|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **BNSF Railway** | Private (Berkshire Hathaway) | 33,400 | Western U.S. | Largest freight railroad; strong intermodal network |
| **CSX Corporation** | ~$65B | 20,000+ | Eastern U.S. | Precision Scheduled Railroading pioneer |
| **Norfolk Southern** | ~$63B | 19,500 | Eastern U.S. | Strong industrial and intermodal focus |
| **Canadian National** | ~$70B | 20,000+ | Canada/U.S. | North-south transcontinental routes |
| **Canadian Pacific Kansas City** | ~$70B | 20,000+ | Canada/U.S./Mexico | Only single-line railroad linking all three countries |

### Competitive Landscape

**Western U.S. Duopoly:** Union Pacific and BNSF Railway share a near-duopoly on freight railroad transportation west of the Mississippi River. They compete for similar routes and customers, with shared trackage rights over thousands of miles.

**Eastern U.S. Duopoly:** Norfolk Southern and CSX have a similar duopoly in the Eastern United States.

### Union Pacific's Key Differentiators

1. **Mexico Gateway Leadership:** Only railroad serving all six major U.S./Mexico gateways with 60% rail market share in Mexico

2. **Network Reach:** Connects all major West Coast and Gulf Coast ports to eastern gateways; links to all Class I Canadian railroads

3. **Population Center Coverage:** Serves many of the fastest-growing U.S. population centers

4. **Technology Investment:** $100M productivity target over three years through AI and technology; leveraging AI training models for intermodal capabilities

5. **Operational Excellence:** Industry-leading operating ratio improvement; strong safety, service, and productivity metrics

6. **Capital Allocation:** Strong shareholder returns through dividends (~45% payout ratio) and buybacks ($4-5B annually)

### 2025 Norfolk Southern Merger

In July 2025, Union Pacific announced an $85 billion merger agreement with Norfolk Southern to create the first single-company transcontinental freight railroad in U.S. history:

- **Combined Network:** ~50,000 miles of track across 43 states
- **Shareholder Vote:** Approved November 14, 2025
- **Strategic Rationale:** Creates coast-to-coast single-line service capability
- **Industry Impact:** Expected to trigger BNSF-CSX merger discussions to maintain competitive balance
