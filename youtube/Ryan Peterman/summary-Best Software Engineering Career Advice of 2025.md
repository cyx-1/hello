# Best Software Engineering Career Advice of 2025

**Video URL:** https://youtu.be/QCBoxmqijic

---

## Executive Summary

This video compiles career advice from CTOs and distinguished engineers at top tech companies (Anthropic, Google, Meta, Amazon). The recurring themes focus on: defining clear personal values before chasing career goals, investing in relationships alongside technical skills, trusting yourself to overcome imposter syndrome, choosing high-growth environments, building reputation and connections, focusing on decision-making over just coding, and maintaining work-life balance to avoid burnout and regret.

---

## Main Topics & Key Insights

### 1. [Know Your Values & What You Actually Want](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=50s)

**Timestamp:** [00:50](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=50s) - [05:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=324s)

**Key Points:**
- Roy Disney quote: "Decisions are easy when your values are clear to you"
- Story of becoming a dev manager at E7/level 67 by age 30, then experiencing depression after "catching the car"
- The "dog that caught the car" problem - achieving goals without thinking about what comes next
- Warren Buffett example: Would you want billions of dollars but be in your late 90s? Is that worth it?
- The danger of peaking early in your career and having 30+ years left with no direction
- Importance of thinking about whether you actually want the destination, not just the journey
- The "sleeping bag business" - working until 11pm while fiancée visited, nearly losing the relationship
- Some reeds bend, some break - recognize what breaks vs what bends in your life
- Getting to E7 at 30 vs 38 doesn't matter in the long arc of a career

**Quote:** "Be sure you actually want the thing you want. Would you want to be Warren Buffett? So many people love the billions of dollars, but he's in his late 90s."

### 2. [Stop People Pleasing - Please Yourself](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=326s)

**Timestamp:** [05:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=326s) - [06:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=389s)

**Key Points:**
- Cultural tracks: do well in school → get job → get married → feeling of being ahead/behind
- Looking for external validation after achievements instead of internal satisfaction
- The ladder analogy: Are you climbing because you want to get to the top, or because the ladder was put in front of you?
- Critical question: Are you doing what you want, or what's expected of somebody else?
- The person you really need to please is yourself

**Quote:** "Are you climbing the ladder because you want to get to the top or are you climbing a ladder because the ladder was put in front of you?"

### 3. [Overcome Imposter Syndrome - Trust Yourself](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=389s)

**Timestamp:** [06:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=389s) - [07:28](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=448s)

**Key Points:**
- Long period of imposter syndrome and anxiety about not doing well
- Built confidence through successful projects and good feedback over time
- Regret about not trusting judgment earlier and having more audacity
- Had ideas but waited too long due to fear of rocking the boat
- Projects that did rock the boat ended up being very successful
- Could have been less anxious and stressed by trusting instincts earlier

**Quote:** "I could have done more if I was willing to trust my judgment a little bit more or have more audacity to push the boundary and try new things."

### 4. [Invest in Relationships Outside of Work](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=448s)

**Timestamp:** [07:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=457s) - [08:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=531s)

**Key Points:**
- Realized only in the last 1.5 years that he was underinvested in friendships and romantic relationships
- Put so much effort into work that personal life suffered
- Spent time roadmapping career progression but didn't apply same rigor to general life progression
- Moving to LA and leaving the tech bubble made the difference - friends outside tech
- Now invests more time and financially in relationships
- Life is much more fulfilled as a result
- Work continues to be great, but now has people to rely on (example: moving apartments)
- These relationships matter more than quick career progression

**Quote:** "I was underinvested in friendships and romantic relationships. You spend all this time road mapping, goaling, checking in with yourself about your career progression. But in terms of like general life progression, I didn't apply nearly that same rigor."

### 5. [Be the Only, Not the Best](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=531s)

**Timestamp:** [08:53](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=533s) - [09:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=578s)

**Key Points:**
- Early career fear of being mediocre at a lot of things
- Looked at friends who were really good at design or better engineers
- The unique skill: ability to negotiate across all those themes (design, engineering, etc.)
- Uncomfortable because unsure if it would pan out - but it did
- Better to be the only person who can do X than to compete to be the best at Y

**Quote:** "Don't be the best, be the only. I see my friends who are really good at design and I'd be like, oh, I wish I could do that. But the thing that I had was ability to negotiate across all those themes."

### 6. [Be Braver & Overcome Fear of Public Speaking](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=578s)

**Timestamp:** [09:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=578s) - [10:50](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=650s)

**Key Points:**
- In high school, deathly afraid of public speaking - would run away from class to avoid it
- Part of 9-to-5 later became public speaking
- The difference: Speaking about something you're passionate about vs. being tested on knowledge
- Pitching a product you care about vs. describing something you have no investment in
- Cold sweats presenting in university, but totally comfortable pitching PostRank product
- If told in college that future holds public speaking, would have thought it was crazy

**Quote:** "Going through high school, I was deathly afraid of public speaking. I would run away from class to avoid it. Later in my career, part of my 9-to-5 became public speaking."

### 7. [Choose High Growth Environments](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=650s)

**Timestamp:** [10:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=652s) - [11:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=687s)

**Key Points:**
- Always prefer high growth companies
- Career ladder vs. escalator metaphor: escalator is moving up for you while you also climb
- Amazon grew 100x while there: 10,000 people to 1 million
- Revenue grew 80x
- Rode the escalator up while also climbing

**Quote:** "People talk about a career ladder. Well, my ladder was always an escalator. I could climb, but it was also moving up for me."

### 8. [Build Relationships & Reputation](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=687s)

**Timestamp:** [11:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=690s) - [12:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=744s)

**Key Points:**
- Jobs are still with other humans
- Great to be an expert and be right, but build skills for relationships
- Make friends, get to know lots of people
- Don't have to be an extrovert - was a classic introvert
- Use online tools like LinkedIn to build reputation from your keyboard
- Amazon called him for the job, not the other way around - that's the goal

**Quote:** "Wake up much sooner to jobs are still with other humans. It's great to be an expert, it's great to be right, but build the skills to have the relationships, make the friends."

### 9. [Focus on Decision-Making, Not Just Coding](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=744s)

**Timestamp:** [12:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=747s) - [13:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=795s)

**Key Points:**
- When someone says "write code to do this," ask: how do they know that's the right code?
- Aim to be the person making decisions about what's the right thing to do
- Having this clarity earlier would have helped with imposter syndrome
- You're more than what you can type on a keyboard
- Your ideas and value are greater than being an output machine
- Focus on being in the rooms that decide what's the right thing to do

**Quote:** "When someone tells you, 'can you write some code to do this?' You should stop and think, how do they know that's the right code to write? You should aim to be the person who's making those decisions."

### 11. [Go Where You're Valued](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=836s)

**Timestamp:** [13:58](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=838s) - [14:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=883s)

**Key Points:**
- Example: Really good at frontend but end up on backend team - performance suffers
- Example: Like working on frameworks but on product team - get bad rating despite landing 500 diffs
- Lean into your strengths
- Management chain or team might not care about your strengths
- Find a team that values what you're good at
- You'll be way happier and it will work much better

**Quote:** "Go where you're valued. Go into the environment in which you're valued and maybe you end up being in an environment you're not valued in for a year, but just recognize that and get out of it."

### 12. [Just Try Stuff - Don't Be Afraid](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=883s)

**Timestamp:** [14:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=886s) - [15:05](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=905s)

**Key Points:**
- Don't be afraid just because it looks hard
- Don't avoid things because other people look much better than you
- They might be better, but you can also get there
- It's not rocket science, it's just computers
- If you like computers, you're pretty well off if you just keep going

**Quote:** "Just try stuff and don't be afraid just because it looks hard or because those other people look much better than you. It's not rocket science. It is just computers."

### 13. [Use Common Sense](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=905s)

**Timestamp:** [15:08](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=908s) - [15:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCBoxmqijic&t=946s)

- Things are "this way because they have been this way"

- In startups: use common sense to figure out what the market wants and what users want
- Trust yourself and develop your common sense

**Quote:** "Just use common sense. There's a lot of stuff in especially big companies that pulls you away from common sense. Things are this way because they have been this way. There's a lot of misaligned incentives."

## Summary of Core Themes

1. **Self-awareness is critical** - Know your values, what you actually want, and whether you're people-pleasing or pursuing your own goals
2. **Relationships matter more than titles** - Invest in friendships, romantic relationships, and professional connections as much as technical skills
3. **Trust yourself earlier** - Overcome imposter syndrome, be braver, try things even when you're scared
4. **Strategic career choices** - Choose high-growth companies, go where you're valued, be the only (not the best)
5. **Beyond technical skills** - Focus on decision-making, build your reputation, develop communication skills
6. **Work-life balance prevents regret** - Don't sacrifice everything for career progression; some reeds break
7. **Common sense over politics** - Don't get lost in organizational dysfunction; trust your judgment
