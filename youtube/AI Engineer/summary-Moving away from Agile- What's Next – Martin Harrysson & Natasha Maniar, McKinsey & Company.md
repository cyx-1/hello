## Executive Summary

McKinsey consultants Martin Harrysson and Natasha Maniar present research on how AI is forcing a paradigm shift in software development, moving beyond traditional Agile methodologies. Based on a survey of 300 enterprises, they reveal that while individual developers see dramatic AI productivity gains, most companies achieve only 5-15% overall improvements due to bottlenecks in team collaboration, code review, and workflow allocation. Top-performing organizations overcome this by implementing "AI-native" workflows with smaller teams (3-5 people vs 8-10), spec-driven development instead of story-driven, continuous planning instead of quarterly sprints, and consolidated roles where engineers orchestrate agents. Success requires comprehensive change management including hands-on upskilling, measurement systems, and incentive structures. The talk provides concrete case studies showing 51% increases in code mergers and 60x increases in agent adoption when these organizational changes are implemented properly.
