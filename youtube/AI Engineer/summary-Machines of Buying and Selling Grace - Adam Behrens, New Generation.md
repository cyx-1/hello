# Machines of Buying and Selling Grace - Adam Behrens, New Generation

**Video URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg

---

## Executive Summary

Two leaders from Sprout Social (Rala and Dibsha) share their real-world experience transforming a SaaS company into an "AI-first" organization. Rather than offering a perfect playbook, they provide a candid look at the messy reality of AI transformation through a three-dimensional framework: Strategy, Ways of Working, and People. The talk emphasizes that AI transformation is fundamentally a cultural shift, not just a technical one, and requires balancing present customer needs with future innovation while navigating constant ambiguity.

---

## Main Topics

### [Introduction: What Does "AI First" Really Mean?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=17s)
**Timestamp: [00:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=17s)**

- The presenters acknowledge that "AI first" is like teenage sex - everyone talks about it but no one really knows how to do it
- AI first means evolving from sprinkling AI features into products to rethinking how you plan, build, and deliver value through an AI lens
- Unlike previous tech shifts (cloud, mobile, DevOps) that disrupted one area at a time, AI disrupts everything simultaneously: product, architecture, people, process, and ethics
- AI transformation is not binary - it's a multi-dimensional journey and evolution, not flipping a switch
- Key distinction: AI-enhanced asks "where can we add intelligence?" while AI-first asks "what new experiences are possible that weren't before?"

### [The Innovator's Dilemma Amplified by AI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=323s)
**Timestamp: [05:23](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=323s)**

- Companies face extreme tension: ship what customers need today while investing in the future
- Overindexing on present = falling behind; overindexing on future = disappointing customers and starving innovation
- AI makes the innovator's dilemma 10x harder - you need the discipline of an enterprise AND the curiosity/nimbleness of a startup running in parallel
- Metaphor: "It's like steering a ship and launching a rocket at the same time"
- Solution: Build a startup inside your own company

### [The Three-Dimensional Framework](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=219s)
**Timestamp: [03:39](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=219s)**

Sprout Social's framework for AI transformation has three key dimensions:

1. **Strategy** - What you prioritize and why
2. **Ways of Working** - How you build, ship, and adapt
3. **People** - How teams evolve and the skills that define success

### [Strategy Shift: From Roadmaps to Ambiguity](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=396s)
**Timestamp: [06:36](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=396s)**

- Moving from deterministic roadmaps (knowing what you're building months ahead) to embracing ambiguity where learning and discovery shape the path
- Three-month roadmaps feel stale in 3 weeks when capabilities evolve constantly
- Design evolution: Customers now expect seamless intelligent systems that stretch across workflows and roles, not just AI features in isolation

### [The "Wufill" Example: Unified AI Experiences](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=447s)
**Timestamp: [07:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=447s)**

- Fictional health tracking app for puppies (customer: "Pineapples the puppy")
- Traditional AI: Separate features (meals, activities, digestive insights) each owned by different teams, giving narrow responses
- AI-first: Unified experience that synthesizes across all data points to provide holistic insights
- Example: When the puppy says "I'm feeling kind of blah," the unified AI response connects new kibble, declining activity, irregular digestion, and new supplements to provide comprehensive guidance
- Key insight: Not just smarter features with AI sprinkled in, but unification to generate broader value

### [Ways of Working: Ritualized Discovery](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=542s)
**Timestamp: [09:02](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=542s)**

- Historical innovation was reactive and ad hoc - someone had an idea, tested in isolation, then moved on
- AI-first requires treating discovery as a repeatable, deliberate process
- Building time into planning cycles for experimentation, hackathons, and learning across various forums
- MVPs are for learning and validating direction, not just launching quickly
- Failure is a feature, not a bug - it drives clarity through ambiguity

### [Process as Product](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=615s)
**Timestamp: [10:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=615s)**

- Old processes were designed for predictable, linear roadmaps with deterministic risks
- In AI-first world where timelines shift and capabilities evolve monthly, old processes don't hold up
- Too many new processes create overhead and drag (Sprout learned this the hard way)
- Solution: Treat process as a product - evaluate against outcomes:
  - Does it create clarity of direction?
  - Does it unblock teams?
  - Does it help make better decisions faster?
- If no, reiterate or cut it entirely
- When purposeful, process gives clarity and flow, becoming an accelerator

### [Smart Velocity Over Speed](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=683s)
**Timestamp: [11:23](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=683s)**

- Speed matters, but only when paired with direction
- Moving fast without clarity = chaos
- Having clarity without momentum = stagnation
- Smart velocity = building muscle to move fast with purpose, clarity, momentum, and adaptability
- Not about shipping for the sake of shipping, but working with intention

### [People: The Cultural Transformation](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=733s)
**Timestamp: [12:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=733s)**

- Becoming AI-first isn't just tech transformation - it's mainly a cultural transformation
- Culture lives in people: how they think, work, lead, and feel
- Need to rethink what great talent looks like across the entire company (not just AI teams)
- Two major shifts at Sprout Social:
  1. How talent is evolving in the AI era
  2. How to scale AI fluency across the organization

### [T-Shaped Talent: The Indiana Jones Model](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=791s)
**Timestamp: [13:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=791s)**

- Previously: Deep specialization was critical; being a generalist/visionary builder was an edge but not essential
- Now: AI depth is still essential, but being a versatile visionary builder has become critical to success
- T-shaped talent: Deep expertise PLUS ability to stretch wide, prototype quickly, collaborate fluidly across silos, and bring end-to-end systems to life
- Indiana Jones metaphor: Combining the professor (deep specialization) with the adventurer (bringing expertise into the jungle to uncover unimaginable possibilities)
- Need pathfinders who can hold deep expertise while forging new ways through shifting terrain where the path doesn't even exist

### [Organization-Wide AI Fluency](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=910s)
**Timestamp: [15:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=910s)**

- You can't go AI-first if the rest of the company is still "AI-last"
- Everyone touches AI, even if not training models or building agents: marketers, designers, PMs, care agents
- Goal: Every team feels empowered to understand AI and confident to build with it
- Investing in org-wide AI fluency through:
  - AI newsletters and podcasts
  - Cross-functional AI show-and-tells
  - Empowering teams to use whatever AI tools help them work smarter
  - Building self-service platforms so product/engineering teams can prototype and ship AI features without deep AI team involvement
- Objective: Make AI thinking and exploration the default, not the exception

### [What Hasn't Changed: The Fundamentals](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=1024s)
**Timestamp: [17:04](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=1024s)**

- Best AI features still solve customer problems - rooted in needs, not novelty
- User experience, performance, reliability, and trust remain non-negotiable
- Human creativity, judgment, and care remain central to leadership, decision-making, and how we show up for teams and customers

### [Advice for AI Leaders](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=1054s)
**Timestamp: [17:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlZz0mDF2eg&t=1054s)**

## Key Takeaways

1. **AI transformation is messy and non-linear** - there's no perfect playbook; every company's journey looks different
2. **It's a cultural shift, not a tech upgrade** - success depends on how people think, work, and evolve
3. **Balance is everything** - serve today's customers while building tomorrow's capabilities
4. **Embrace ambiguity** - rigid roadmaps don't work when the landscape changes monthly
5. **Unified experiences > isolated features** - AI-first means connecting across workflows and teams
6. **Process should accelerate, not constrain** - evaluate processes like products
7. **Everyone needs AI fluency** - not just specialists, but across the entire organization
8. **T-shaped talent is critical** - deep expertise combined with versatility and vision
9. **Fundamentals still matter** - customer problems, UX, trust, and human judgment remain central
10. **You're shaping history** - this is a human revolution, not just a technological one
